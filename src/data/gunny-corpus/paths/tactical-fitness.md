# MODULE 1 — FOUNDATIONAL TACTICAL FITNESS

## 1.1 Population & Goal
Any tactical athlete with a deconditioned, undertrained, or unstructured base. Includes new recruits, future selection candidates 12+ months out, civilians transitioning into tactical roles (FBI applicants, police academy candidates, fire candidates), and operators returning from injury or extended layoff. Goal is a **broad, durable physical base** capable of supporting any subsequent specialization, with primary emphasis on injury resistance, posterior-chain strength, aerobic base, work capacity, and load-bearing tolerance.

## 1.2 Programming Principles
- **Strength is the foundation.** Per Rob Shaul (MTI): *"Strength is the key to durability and longevity."* NSCA TSAC consensus echoes this — tactical populations need **complex multi-joint, posterior-chain-dominant** strength as the priority for both performance and injury reduction.
- **Aerobic base before intensity.** Tactical Barbell's **Base Building (BB) phase** explicitly de-emphasizes max strength for the first 4–5 weeks to build cardiovascular volume; this is the most evidence-aligned approach for previously untrained or detrained athletes.
- **Concurrent multi-modal training is acceptable** for tactical athletes (Fluid Periodization), but for true beginners, **simplify**: 2 strength + 2–3 aerobic + 1 mobility per week.
- **Train movement quality, don't pre-screen it.** MTI rejects "the tyranny of the FMS"; movement quality is built through high-frequency submaximal exposure.
- **Volume caps for calisthenics** to prevent overuse: NSW PTG specifies *no more than 200 push-ups or sit-ups, and no more than 50 pull-ups in a single day; no more than 1,000 push-ups or sit-ups, and no more than 250 pull-ups per week*.

> **Methodology disagreement:** MTI runs concurrent multi-modal training from day one (Fluid Periodization). Tactical Barbell de-emphasizes max strength during weeks 1–5 of Base Building and rebuilds aerobically first. For previously sedentary populations, **TB's BB approach is more conservative and lower-injury-risk**; for already-active recreational lifters/runners, MTI's concurrent approach works well.

## 1.3 Sample 8-Week Template (TB-style Base Building, with MTI exercise selection)

**Schedule:** 5 days/week. Mon/Wed/Fri = strength-endurance + LSS option. Tue/Thu = LSS endurance.

**Weeks 1–4 (no max-strength barbell work):**

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | SE Circuit Alpha + 30-min LSS run |
| Tue | LSS run/ruck 30–45 min @ HR 120–150 (Z2) |
| Wed | SE Circuit Bravo + 30-min row/bike |
| Thu | LSS swim or bike 40 min (active recovery mode) |
| Fri | SE Circuit Alpha + 200m sandbag carry intervals |
| Sat | Long ruck or hike, 45–60 min @ 20–25 lb |
| Sun | Off |

**SE Circuit Alpha (TB-derived; 3 rounds, minimal rest within rounds, 2–3 min rest between rounds):**
- 2-hand kettlebell swing × 10/20/30 (weeks 1/2/3, then repeat wave)
- Goblet squat × 10/20/30
- Push-up × 10/20/30
- Bent-over row (light barbell, 95 lb / 65 lb F) × 10/20/30
- Hanging leg raise × 10/15/20
- Hyperextension × 10/15/20

**Weeks 5–8 (reintroduce max strength via TB Fighter template):**
Two strength days (Mon + Fri); all 4 main lifts each session (Back Squat, Bench Press, Weighted Pull-Up, Conventional Deadlift). **Use TB Fighter wave on Training Max (TM = 0.9 × 1RM):**

| Week | Sets × Reps | % TM |
|------|-------------|------|
| 5 | 3 × 5 | 70% |
| 6 | 3 × 5 | 80% |
| 7 | 3 × 3 | 90% |
| 8 | 3 × 5 | 75% (deload/test) |

**Conditioning weeks 5–8:**
- 1× HIC/week — start with 600m repeat resets: sprint 600m max effort, full 3–5 min recovery, 3–4 rounds
- 2× LSS endurance (run/ruck/row), 45–75 min
- 1× Saturday long ruck, 45 lb, 60–90 min @ 17–18 min/mile

## 1.4 Relevant Assessments
- **MTI Tactical Athlete Work Capacity Assessment** (3 × 3 min shuttle, 25-lb vest) — see Appendix A.
- **MTI Relative Strength Assessment baseline:** 185 lb bench, 200 lb back squat, 250 lb deadlift, 8 strict pull-ups (male tactical floor per Shaul).
- **1.5-mile run** (Cooper) ≤14:00 male / ≤16:00 female as a foundational target.
- **45-lb ruck, 3 miles in 45 min** (USFS Arduous Pack Test pace).

## 1.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The most common Module 1 failure is **doing too much too fast** — typically running 4–5 days/week while simultaneously starting a 5/3/1 strength program and adding HIIT, then quitting at week 6 from cumulative fatigue or injury. Push-up and sit-up volume in particular accumulates damage to shoulders and hip flexors when prescribed daily without tissue tolerance. **Coaching cue: build aerobic volume first, then layer strength.** Use heart-rate caps (≤150 bpm on Z2 days) to enforce easy pace; nasal breathing is a useful field check. For squats and deadlifts in deconditioned athletes, regress to **goblet squats and trap-bar deadlifts** before barbell back squat; prioritize hip hinge pattern via Romanian deadlift drilling at light loads.

## 1.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for severely deconditioned (BMI >30 or >12-month sedentary):** drop to 3 days/week for 4 weeks. Walk-only LSS at 30 min building to 60 min. Bodyweight-only SE circuits at 5/10/15 reps (TB Bravo circuits). MTI's "Bodyweight Beginner" or "Humility" plans serve here.
- **Progression to Module 2 / 4 / 8:** when athlete passes the entry assessments (push-ups ≥40, pull-ups ≥6, 1.5-mile <13:00, 45-lb ruck 3 mi <45 min) and completes 8 weeks consecutive without missed sessions.

# MODULE 2 — ACTIVE DUTY OPERATOR MAINTENANCE

## 2.1 Population & Goal
Already-fit operators (SOF, SWAT, fire engine company crew, conventional combat-arms NCOs) in-garrison between deployments or major events. PFT scores are 280+ on Marine PFT, 450+ on Army AFT, or equivalent. Goal is **maintain broad readiness while avoiding overuse and protecting joints for a long career**. Time is constrained (45–60 min/session), missions interrupt training, and training must coexist with mandatory unit PT and shift work.

## 2.2 Programming Principles
- **Year-round readiness is required**; tactical athletes have *no off-season* (Shaul, MTI).
- **Strength priority with maintenance conditioning** — Tactical Barbell's **Black Protocol** (Operator template + 2–3 HIC/week + 1 E every other week) is the closest fit for SWAT, LE patrol, and conventional combat NCOs not in deployment ramp.
- **Concurrent training caveat:** schedule heavy strength and long endurance on different days when possible; if same-day, lift first. Minimum 3 hours between sessions; 6+ hours preferred (NSCA TSAC consensus, Eddens et al. 2018 meta-analysis).
- **Ruck under operational loads** (45–65 lb) at least every other week to maintain load-bearing tolerance.
- **Plan unload weeks** every 4–6 weeks to manage cumulative fatigue (MTI: *"the burden of constant fitness"*).

## 2.3 Sample Weekly Template (TB Operator + MTI Chassis Integrity)

**Strength: TB Operator template, 3 days/week, 6-week wave on TM (TM = 0.9 × 1RM).**

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | Operator MS — Back Squat / Bench Press / Weighted Pull-Up |
| Tue | HIC — 600m resets (5 × 600m, full 3–5 min recovery) OR Apex hill sprints |
| Wed | Operator MS — Back Squat / Bench Press / Weighted Pull-Up |
| Thu | MTI Chassis Integrity grind, 20 min (e.g., ART circuit: 6× 1-sided DL + 3× SB keg lift @ 60 lb + 6× SB getup) |
| Fri | Operator MS — Bench Press / Deadlift / Weighted Pull-Up |
| Sat | E session (45–90 min easy ruck @ 45 lb or LSS run) — alternates weeks |
| Sun | Off |

**TB Operator wave:**

| Week | Sets × Reps | % TM |
|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | 3–5 × 5 | 70% |
| 2 | 3–5 × 5 | 80% |
| 3 | 3–5 × 3 | 90% |
| 4 | 3–5 × 5 | 75% |
| 5 | 3–5 × 5 | 85% |
| 6 | 3–5 × 1–2 | 95% (test/retest 1RM end of week 6) |

**Rest between sets:** 2 min minimum; up to 5 min on weeks 3, 5, 6.

> **Methodology disagreement:** MTI rejects 1RM-percentage programming; the **Big 24** uses 3RM + absolute pound progressions instead. For an operator with reliable barbell loading and a free space to test, MTI's Big 24 (8×3 reps, 4-progression cycle) is a high-yield 5-week strength block — see Module 7 for full protocol. Use TB Operator for sustained year-round programming; drop in MTI Big 24 as a 5-week strength push every 6–9 months.

## 2.4 Relevant Assessments
Quarterly: re-test 1RMs (squat/bench/deadlift), Operator Ugly or MTI Tactical Athlete Work Capacity, 1.5-mile run, 45-lb 3-mile ruck. Every 6 months: full unit-relevant test (PFT, AFT, CPAT timed run, etc.). Assessment timing should not interfere with mission — schedule for in-garrison weeks.

## 2.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The most frequent failure is **chasing PRs in every domain simultaneously** — operators try to add deadlift weight while improving 5K time and increasing ruck load in the same month, then injure low back, knee, or shoulder. **Coaching cue: pick one progression at a time** — strength OR work capacity OR endurance — and let the others maintain. The shoulder/lumbopelvic/knee triad accounts for the majority of tactical injuries (NSCA TSAC; AFSOC injury data showing shoulder 20.9%, lumbopelvic 15.5%); program **antagonist balance**: every push has a pull, every flexion has an extension, every rotation has an anti-rotation. Use MTI's Chassis Integrity (rotation/anti-rotation/extension/flexion/total) as the primary midsection work — not crunches.

## 2.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for high op-tempo / shift work:** drop to MTI's "Busy Operator" model — 5×/week, 30–50 min sessions, density-based strength + short conditioning. Keep one heavy strength day per week, one HIC, one ruck.
- **Progression to Module 3 (pre-deployment):** when a deployment is announced and 6–12 weeks remain, drop out of Module 2 and into Module 3.

# MODULE 3 — PRE-DEPLOYMENT RAMP-UP

## 3.1 Population & Goal
Operator with a **known deployment or validation event** 6–12 weeks out. Already conditioned (Module 2 baseline). Goal is to **peak mission-direct fitness** in a narrow window — load tolerance under kit, work capacity, repeat sprint capacity, ruck speed at operational loads, and durability under sleep deprivation/heat/altitude.

## 3.2 Programming Principles
- **Mission-direct exercise selection** (MTI first principle): identify the demands of the deployment environment (urban, mountain, maritime), train those demands directly.
- **Build backward from the event:** set end-of-cycle goals (e.g., 12-mile ruck @ 65 lb in 2:45, 20-min Operator Ugly score), progress backward through 8 weeks.
- **Volume peaks in weeks 4–6, tapers in weeks 7–8.**
- **Heat/altitude/kit acclimation** in final 2–3 weeks: train in IOTV/plate carrier (~25–35 lb), in expected climate.
- **Reduce technical lift volume** in the final 2 weeks; emphasize movement under load.

## 3.3 Sample 8-Week Template (mountain/austere infantry deployment example)

**Schedule:** 6 days/week. Strength 2× (Mon, Thu), Work Cap 2× (Tue, Fri), Endurance 1–2× (Wed, Sat).

**Weeks 1–2 (build base under load):**
- Mon: Front Squat 5×3 + Bench Press 5×3 + Weighted Pull-Up — heavy density
- Tue: 25-lb vest 25m shuttle work cap, 4×3 min on 1 min rest
- Wed: 60-min Z2 ruck @ 45 lb
- Thu: Hinge Lift (trap bar deadlift) 5×3 + Push Press + Bent-over Row
- Fri: MTI ART Chassis Integrity grind, 20 min + 200m sandbag carry intervals
- Sat: Long ruck 6 mi @ 45 lb, 15 min/mile pace

**Weeks 3–6 (peak work capacity + ruck pace):**
- Tue: 8 × 60-sec / 60-sec shuttle work cap @ 25-lb vest (4 rounds wk 3, 6 rounds wk 4, 8 rounds wk 5)
- Wed: 25-lb vest 3-mile run, target ≤27:00
- Sat: Long ruck progression — 8 mi → 10 mi → 12 mi @ 60 lb, target 13–14 min/mile

**Weeks 7–8 (taper + simulate):**
- Drop strength volume to 60% normal; 1×3 working set top single
- Replace work cap with simulation: kit run + casualty drag + sandbag carry circuit
- Final 12-mile ruck assessment week 7; rest week 8 except mobility

## 3.4 Relevant Assessments
- 12-mile ruck @ 60 lb in <3:00 (15 min/mile floor, 13–14 min/mile competitive — SFAS-aligned)
- 3-mile kit run (25-lb vest) ≤27:00
- Operator Ugly retest
- AFT/PFT to standard pre-deployment

## 3.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The most common Module 3 mistake is **adding volume without removing volume** — operator stacks pre-deployment ruck volume on top of normal Module 2 strength, then accumulates lower-extremity overuse injuries (stress reactions, IT band, plantar fasciitis) at exactly the wrong time. **Coaching cue: when ruck volume goes up, strength volume comes down.** Specifically, drop one strength day during peak ruck weeks (4–6). Also: blistering and foot care. Break in the boots and socks that will be worn in theater 6+ weeks before deployment, on long rucks, with the actual sock rotation.

## 3.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for mid-cycle injury:** drop to single-leg / unloaded work; maintain upper-body strength; replace ruck with weighted-vest hike or step-ups.
- **Progression to deployment maintenance:** Module 2 Busy Operator template adapted to in-theater environment (often ≤45 min, no Olympic lifts, KB and bodyweight + sandbag).

# MODULE 4 — SELECTION PREP, GENERAL (MOUNTAIN ATHLETE / RUCK-HEAVY)

## 4.1 Population & Goal
Candidate 12+ months out from a ruck-dominant selection — **SFAS, MARSOC A&S, Recon BRC, USMS SOG, FBI HRT, BORTAC, Ranger School (general track)**. Already past Module 1 baseline. Goal: build the rucking, running, calisthenics, and chassis integrity capacity needed to **survive the volume of selection** with margin to spare.

## 4.2 Programming Principles
- **Rucking is the strongest single physical predictor of selection outcomes.** Per cited 2019 SFAS data: top quartile of ruck performance had ~67% selection rate, bottom quartile <10%.
- **Long aerobic base required** — minimum 12 months of consistent running before selection (USAJFKSWCS SFAS Prep Handbook recommendation).
- **Strength matters — don't drop strength to chase ruck speed.** MTI mini-study: hybrid (2 strength / 3 endurance) produced +9.4% ruck speed; strength-emphasis (3/2) +8.5%; endurance-only (1/4) only +3.2%.
- **MTI's RBSTP (Ruck-Based Selection Training Plan)** and the **SFAS 50-Week Pipeline** (7 sequential plans: Military On-Ramp → Humility → Big 24 → Fortitude → Valor → Resilience → RBSTP) are the most-validated public templates.
- **2:1 run/walk method** (run 2 min, walk 1 min) for rucking — MTI mini-study showed superior pacing and lower mental load over 6 miles at 60 lb vs continuous ruck-running.

## 4.3 Sample 16-Week Template (compressed RBSTP-style)

**Phase 1 (weeks 1–4) — strength + run base:**
- Mon: MTI Big 24 Day 1 (Back Squat 8×3 RM-based + Walking Lunge + Hang Squat Clean 9×2)
- Tue: Run intervals — 6 × 800m @ 5K pace; recovery jog
- Wed: Big 24 Day 2 (Bench Press 8×3 + Weighted Pull-Up + Push Press)
- Thu: Easy 5-mile run + 20-min Chassis Integrity grind
- Fri: Big 24 Day 1 repeat
- Sat: Ruck 4 mi @ 45 lb, target 14 min/mile — assessment week 1, repeat as intervals weeks 2–4
- Sun: Off

**Phase 2 (weeks 5–10) — ruck volume + work capacity:**
- Mon: Strength density (5 rounds × 4 reps every 90 sec @ 80% 1RM Trap-Bar DL + 80% 1RM Bench)
- Tue: 25-lb vest shuttle work cap, 4 × 3 min
- Wed: 6-mile ruck assessment + 2-mile interval @ 20% faster than per-mile pace, 45 lb
- Thu: 5-mile run @ tempo + Chassis grind
- Fri: Sandbag work cap (5× SBGU @ 60 lb + 200m run with sandbag + 200m unloaded, AMRAP 20 min)
- Sat: Long ruck 8–12 mi @ 45–60 lb, 14–15 min/mile target
- Sun: Off

**Phase 3 (weeks 11–14) — selection-specific:**
- Daily 2-a-days. Morning: run or ruck intervals. Afternoon: strength + chassis or work capacity.
- 12-mile ruck assessment @ 45 lb + 10-lb rifle every 2 weeks; intervals = 4-mile repeats at 20% faster than per-mile pace.
- Long Saturday rucks build to 18 miles @ 60–65 lb.

**Phase 4 (weeks 15–16) — taper:**
- Volume cut 40–50%. Maintain 1 strength session, 1 work cap, 1 short ruck.
- Foot care, gear preparation, sleep prioritization.

## 4.4 Relevant Assessments
Appendix A SFAS / MARSOC / Recon standards. Specifically:
- 12-mile ruck @ 35 lb + 10-lb rifle in **≤3:00** (Ranger School / SFAS standard)
- 5-mile run ≤40:00 (RASP / Ranger School)
- ACFT ≥350 (combat standard) or AFT-equivalent
- Combat Water Survival Test (15-m uniform-and-rifle swim, 3-m blindfold drop, equipment ditch)

## 4.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The most common selection-prep failure is **rucking too heavy too soon**, leading to lower-extremity stress reactions in months 2–4 of preparation. **Coaching cue: build pace at lower load before adding load.** Start at 45 lb dry, get to 14 min/mile for 12 miles, *then* add load to 60 lb. The second most common failure is **running form breakdown under fatigue** — adopt the FM 7-22 Pose Method drilling (Pose → Fall → Pull, cadence ≥180 spm) and practice it every week. The third is **inadequate posterior chain and grip**: deadlift, RDL, and farmer's carries are non-negotiable — selection is full of carrying awkward, heavy objects.

## 4.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression** for foot/lower-extremity injury: substitute weighted-vest step-ups (16–18″ box, 25–45 lb vest), rower, or pool running. Maintain upper body and chassis volume.
- **Progression to Module 6:** if candidate is targeting Ranger School or SFAS specifically and is within 8 weeks of report date, drop into Module 6.

# MODULE 5 — SELECTION PREP, WATER/SWIM HEAVY

## 5.1 Population & Goal
Candidate 12–24 months out from **BUD/S, SWCC, Recon RTAP/BRC, Air Force PJ/CCT/SR/STO Special Warfare pipeline, USCG Aviation Survival Technician (Rescue Swimmer)**. Goal: build the **swimming, breath-holding, water confidence, and in-water power-endurance** to survive the water portions of selection, while maintaining ruck/run/strength baselines.

## 5.2 Programming Principles
- **Swim volume must be high and frequent.** NSW PTG explicitly recommends 4–6 swim sessions per week in the prep window.
- **Combat sidestroke (CSS) or breaststroke** is the BUD/S standard; freestyle is faster but not the test stroke. Train CSS specifically.
- **Underwater swims and water confidence** — drown-proofing and 50m underwaters are the top two attrition events at BUD/S First Phase and AF Special Warfare pipeline.
- **Fin work** — 1,000–2,000m fin swims weekly for BUD/S; **2× 25m underwater + 500m surface** for AF Special Warfare PAST/IFT.
- **Maintain rucking** — many water-track selections (Recon, MARSOC, BRC) include 12-mile ruck events.
- **MTI's BUD/S V3 Plan** (8 weeks, 6 days/week, 2-a-days, includes Navy EOD) and **BUD/S Pipeline** (9 plans, 55–58 weeks: On-Ramp → Humility → Valor + Swim Improvement → US Navy PST Plan → Operator Pentathlon → Fortitude → BUD/S V3 → Recovery) are the most validated structures.

## 5.3 Sample 12-Week Template (BUD/S-track)

**Daily structure: AM swim, PM strength/run/ruck.**

**Phase 1 (weeks 1–4) — base building, swim emphasis:**
- Mon AM: 1500m CSS continuous + 4 × 50m underwater | PM: TB Operator strength (squat/bench/WPU)
- Tue AM: Fin swim 1000m + treading 10 min (no hands) | PM: Run 5 mi + 50 push-ups every mile
- Wed AM: 500-yd PST swim time trial weekly | PM: Bodyweight density (PST max sets push-up/sit-up/pull-up)
- Thu AM: Swim 2000m varied stroke + buddy tow practice | PM: 4-mi ruck @ 45 lb
- Fri AM: 2× 25m underwater + 1500m CSS | PM: TB Operator strength (DL/press/WPU)
- Sat: Long open-water swim 1.5–2 mi + light run
- Sun: Off

**Phase 2 (weeks 5–8) — peak volume:**
- Add 4-mile timed run (boots/sneakers per current PST policy) weekly, target ≤28:00
- Build 2-mile fin swim toward BUD/S 75-min standard
- Add 50m underwater + drown-proofing practice weekly
- Maintain 1× weekly 6-mi ruck @ 45 lb

**Phase 3 (weeks 9–12) — PST mastery + recovery:**
- Mock PST every 2 weeks, target competitive scores (500-yd swim ≤9:00, push-ups ≥100, sit-ups ≥100, pull-ups ≥20, 1.5-mi run ≤9:30)
- Reduce strength volume; maintain 2× per week
- Increase pool comp practice and mental skills (CO₂ tolerance, breath-holds)

## 5.4 Relevant Assessments
See Appendix A. Key:
- **BUD/S PST competitive** — see table in Appendix A.
- **AF Special Warfare IFT** — pull-ups ≥13, sit-ups ≥70, push-ups ≥70, 1.5-mi <9:50, 500m surface swim <11:00, 2× 25m underwater pass.
- **Recon RTAP** — 3-mi run ≤22:30, 500m swim in cammies ≤20:00, 8 pull-ups, 25m underwater crossover, 12-ft tower jump, 5-min trouser flotation, 30-min water tread.
- **USCG AST** — 12-min crawl swim ≥500 yds, 25-yd underwater swim, 200-yd buddy tow.

## 5.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The most common water-track failure is **arriving with insufficient swim volume** — candidates with strong land fitness but only 1–2 swims/week wash out at BUD/S First Phase or PJ Indoc water con. **Coaching cue: 4–6 swim sessions per week is the minimum prep dose.** Second pitfall: **kicking with the legs** in CSS instead of using the scissor kick — CSS efficiency comes from glide and a strong scissor, not turnover. Third: **panicking on underwaters** — train CO₂ tolerance with progressive breath-holds and prone push-up holds with held breath, *not* with maximal voluntary apnea (dangerous shallow-water blackout risk).

> **Safety note:** Hypoxic / breath-hold pool training has caused multiple shallow-water blackout deaths. **Never train underwater swims alone**, and never push to consciousness limits. AF Special Warfare and BUD/S pre-courses now restrict hypoxic training due to historical fatalities.

## 5.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for limited pool access:** prioritize fin work in any open water (lake, ocean), use rowing erg as cardio/upper-body substitute, simulate buddy tow with sled drags.
- **Progression to NSW Prep School (8-week course at Great Lakes for SEAL contract):** the PST exit standard is 1000m fin swim ≤20:00 and 4-mi run ≤31:00 — train these specifically in final 6 weeks.

# MODULE 6 — SELECTION PREP, RANGER SCHOOL / SFAS SPECIFICS

## 6.1 Population & Goal
Candidate within **8–12 weeks** of Ranger School, SFAS, RASP 1/2, or 75th Pre-RASP. Has completed Module 4 baseline. Goal is **specific peaking** for the first-week cuts that account for the majority of attrition at these schools (RPA, 12-mile ruck, 5-mile run, land nav).

## 6.2 Programming Principles
- **RAP Week eats most candidates.** Per FY2015 data, 60% of all Ranger School failures occur in RAP; 35% (862 students) failed the Ranger Physical Assessment alone. **Train the test.**
- **The Ranger Physical Assessment (RPA):** 49 push-ups in 2 min (chest-to-ground form), 59 sit-ups in 2 min, 6 dead-hang chin-ups, 5-mile run ≤40:00.
- **The 12-mile foot march:** 35-lb dry ruck + weapon (8 quarts water) ≤3:00 (15 min/mile pace floor). Safety cuts at 1:40 (mile 6) and 2:08 (mile 8).
- **For SFAS:** ACFT must pass Day 1 (specific minimums: ≥28 HR push-ups, ≥6 pull-ups, 2-mile run ≤15:12); cadre indicators of success: sub-13:32 2-mi, 30+ HR push-ups, 12+ pull-ups, sub-15-min/mile ruck pace.
- **Land navigation** is the #1 cited drop event at SFAS — physical prep is necessary but not sufficient; must train day/night individual nav with full ruck.
- **MTI's Ranger School Plan** (8 weeks, 6 days/week, V3+ April 2025) and **MTI Ranger Pipeline** (42 weeks: On-Ramp + Humility + Fortitude + Valor + Gratitude + Ranger Plan) are the most-validated public templates.

## 6.3 Sample 8-Week Ranger School / RASP Template

**Schedule: 6 days/week. RPA + 12-mile ruck assessed in weeks 1, 3, 6, 8.**

| Day | AM | PM |
|-----|-----|-----|
| Mon | Density push-ups (10 sets × 60% max, 1 min rest) + density sit-ups | Heavy strength: Trap Bar DL 5×3 @ 85% 1RM + Bench 5×3 |
| Tue | 5-mile run intervals (assessment-paced 800m repeats) | Pull-up density (5 × 60% max, 2 min rest) |
| Wed | Long ruck @ 35 lb (build 6 → 8 → 10 → 12 mi over weeks 1–6) | Mobility / Chassis Integrity |
| Thu | RPA push-up + sit-up race-pace practice (2-min for-time) | Ruck intervals: 4 × 1-mile @ 12 min/mile w/ 35 lb |
| Fri | 5-mile timed run race-pace (target ≤37:30 by week 6) | Front squat 5×3 + Push press 5×3 |
| Sat | Assessment day weeks 1, 3, 6, 8: RPA + 12-mile ruck OR steady long ruck | Rest |
| Sun | Off | Off |

**RPA / 12-mile ruck assessment specifics:**
- 12-mile ruck: 35-lb dry sack + 10-lb dummy rifle/sledgehammer, full uniform/boots
- Practice the **2:1 run/walk method** (2 min run, 1 min walk) for ruck intervals — empirically faster and lower mental load
- Track per-mile splits; intervals = 4-mile repeats at 20% faster than assessment per-mile pace

## 6.4 Relevant Assessments
- **Ranger School RPA:** 49/59/6/40:00 — see Appendix A
- **75th Ranger Pre-RASP:** 53 push-ups, 63 sit-ups, 4 pull-ups, 2-mi ≤14:30, 6-mi ruck ≤1:30 @ 35 lb
- **SFAS Day 1 minimums:** 28 HR push-ups, 6 pull-ups, 2-mi run ≤15:12 (ACFT pass)
- **CWST:** 15-m uniform-rifle swim, 3-m blindfold drop, equipment ditch underwater

## 6.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The single most common Ranger School failure is **push-up form breakdown** at the RPA — chest must clear ground and lockout must be full; cadre will not count partial reps. **Coaching cue: practice the actual cadence, including a 2-min cap, with strict cadre form, weekly.** Second: **ruck pace mismanagement** — candidates start at 13 min/mile, blow up at mile 6, finish at 16. Train 14:00–14:30 even pacing. Third: **no swim prep** — candidates who never train CWST elements often fail the equipment ditch under water.

For SFAS specifically: **land nav is the primary attrition driver and is rarely trained physically.** Get a lensatic compass and protractor, get to terrain (Camp Mackall, Camp Dawson, or any forested mil reservation that allows access), and run individual point courses with full ruck in day and night conditions. Mountain Tactical Institute and Building the Elite both publish nav-prep guidance worth studying.

## 6.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for late injury:** if a candidate is 3 weeks out and accumulates a stress reaction, drop ruck volume but maintain rucking (lower load 25 lb), maintain run volume on alternate surface (track, treadmill), and protect the test events.
- **Post-completion progression:** drop into Module 2 (Operator Maintenance) within 2 weeks of return-to-duty.

# MODULE 7 — PFT/AFT/PRT MAX-OUT PROGRAMMING

## 7.1 Population & Goal
Active duty service member who **needs to peak a specific service fitness test** — Marine PFT/CFT, Army AFT, Navy PRT, Air Force PFRA, Coast Guard PRP, or service academy PFT. 8–12 weeks out from test date. Goal: maximize point-scored events to reach max score (300/300 PFT, 500/500 AFT, Outstanding-High PRT, ≥90 PFRA).

## 7.2 Programming Principles
- **Train the actual test events** at the actual cadence, with the actual rest, in mock-test format every 2–3 weeks.
- **Calisthenics density progressions** (volume across multiple sub-max sets) build peak rep counts faster than max-effort training. NSW PTG cap: 200 push-ups / 200 sit-ups / 50 pull-ups per day; 1000/1000/250 per week.
- **Run pace work** — for sub-13:30 2-mi or sub-9:30 1.5-mi, mile repeats at goal pace and 800m intervals at faster-than-goal are essential.
- **MTI's AFT Plan** (current V3+) uses Hybrid Big 24/Density progression for trap-bar deadlift (8×3 with 2-min cap on heavy working sets). The **Marine PFT Plan**, **MARSOC Prep Plan**, and **Operator Pentathlon** are also relevant.
- **Body composition matters** — for ACFT/AFT and Marine PFT, lighter body weight at the same strength means higher run scores; for max-strength events, mass helps. For most operators, optimize the run.

## 7.3 Sample 8-Week Templates

### A) Marine PFT Max-Out (Pull-ups + Plank + 3-mile run)

**Goal: 23 pull-ups (max), 3:45 plank (max), 18:00 3-mile run (max) — 300 total.**

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | Pull-up density (5 × 60% max, 90 sec rest) + 3-mile easy run |
| Tue | Run intervals: 6 × 800m @ 5:30 pace (sub-18 3-mi target = 6:00/mi) |
| Wed | Plank density (5 × 60% max time, 60 sec rest) + bench/row strength |
| Thu | 3-mile tempo run @ 6:15/mi |
| Fri | Pull-up max test (every 2 weeks); otherwise weighted pull-up 5×3 |
| Sat | Long run 5–7 mi easy |
| Sun | Off |

**Pull-up density progression:** if max = 18, perform 5 sets × 11 with 90 sec rest. Increase by 1 rep/set per week.

### B) Army AFT Max-Out (5 events; 500 max, 350 combat standard)

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | Trap-Bar Deadlift Hybrid Big 24/Density (8×3, cap 2 min on heavy sets, 5 working sets at 3RM-load progressions) + HR push-up density |
| Tue | SDC simulation: 5×50m sled drag/lateral/KB carry/sprint, full effort, 5 min rest, 4 rounds |
| Wed | Plank density + Bench Press 5×3 |
| Thu | 2-mile run intervals (4×800m @ goal pace) |
| Fri | Repeat Mon (deadlift cycle) |
| Sat | Long run 4–5 mi easy + Standing Power Throw practice (10-lb medicine ball, 10 throws) — note: SPT removed from AFT June 2025 but useful for general power |
| Sun | Off |

**3RM Hybrid Big 24/Density (MTI):** Establish 3RM. 8 sets of 3 reps. Sets 1–3 = warm-up. Sets 4–8 = working sets, progressively at 3RM-15 lb / 3RM-10 lb / 3RM-5 lb / 3RM, with 2-min cap on heavy sets.

### C) Navy PRT Outstanding-High (push-ups + plank + 1.5-mi run)

Goal (M 17–19): 92 push-ups, 3:24 plank, 1.5-mi run 8:15.
- Push-up density 4×/week (5 sets × 60% max, 60 sec rest)
- Plank density 3×/week (5 × 60% max time)
- Run intervals 2×/week + 1 long run + 1 mock 1.5-mi every 2 weeks

## 7.4 Relevant Assessments
See Appendix A for full scoring tables (ACFT/AFT, Marine PFT/CFT, Navy PRT, Air Force PFRA, Coast Guard PRP).

## 7.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The most frequent max-out failure is **over-trained calisthenics** — daily push-up sets to failure cause chronic shoulder pain and *reduced* max within 4 weeks. **Coaching cue: density not failure.** Train at 60–70% of max in 5 sets, not 1 set to failure. Second: **race-pace neglect** — candidates train tempo and easy runs but never run at goal-pace under mock-test conditions. Practice the actual 2-mile or 1.5-mile or 3-mile event at race pace at least every 2 weeks. Third: **plank overuse** — daily 4-min planks cause anterior hip pain and back strain. Use density (5 sets at submax time) and let plank max emerge.

For pull-ups specifically, follow **StrongFirst's Greasing the Groove (GTG)**: scattered short sets through the day, never to failure, or **Pavel's ROP ladder protocol** (1,2,3,4,5 rungs × 5 ladders). Both produce more pull-up volume with less local fatigue than max-set training.

## 7.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for failed event:** if a single event is failing (e.g., 1.5-mi run), drop other events to maintenance and build only the failing event for 4–6 weeks before reintegrating.
- **Progression to combat standard or unit prep:** once max score achieved and stable, drop into Module 2 (operator maintenance) and protect fitness with periodic re-tests.

# MODULE 8 — CPAT PREPARATION (FIREFIGHTER CANDIDATE)

## 8.1 Population & Goal
Civilian firefighter candidate 8–16 weeks out from CPAT. Often coming from variable fitness backgrounds. Goal: **pass CPAT in ≤10:20** wearing 50-lb vest, complete 8 sequential events without DQ.

## 8.2 Programming Principles
- **Stair Climb is the #1 CPAT failure point.** It accounts for the majority of failures because most candidates undertrain it. Train StepMill 3+×/week with weighted vest.
- **Train under load.** All CPAT events occur in the 50-lb vest; train upper-body work, stair climb, and forcible-entry simulation in vest from week 1.
- **Posterior chain and grip** are decisive — hose drag, equipment carry, ceiling pull, and rescue drag all demand both.
- **Aerobic base supports recovery** between events (only 85-foot walk = ~20 sec).
- **IAFF/IAFC publishes a recommended 8–12 week prep program** at iaff.org/cpat. NFPA 1583 fitness program elements (aerobic, body composition, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility) are the in-service framework.

## 8.3 Sample 12-Week CPAT Template

**Schedule: 5 days/week.**

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | StepMill 60 steps/min @ 50-lb vest + 25-lb shoulder load — 3 × 3 min, 90 sec rest |
| Tue | Strength: deadlift 5×5 + farmer carry 4×100 ft (heavy) + push press 5×5 |
| Wed | Hose drag simulation (sled drag, 75-ft drags + 50-ft hand-over-hand pull from kneel) + ceiling-breach simulation (overhead push 50 lb × 60 reps + lat pulldown 80 lb × 100 reps) |
| Thu | StepMill intervals + sledgehammer tire strikes (10-lb sledge, 100 reps in 2 min) |
| Fri | Full CPAT mock simulation (every 2 weeks); otherwise: ladder raise practice + 165-lb dummy drag intervals |
| Sat | Long ruck or hike 60 min @ 45 lb (aerobic base) |
| Sun | Off |

**Stair climb progression:**
- Week 1–2: 50-lb vest only, 3×3 min
- Week 3–4: Add 12.5 lb shoulder weights (62.5 total), 3×3 min
- Week 5–8: 75 lb total (50-lb vest + 25-lb shoulders), 3×3 min — full CPAT load
- Week 9–12: Maintain 75 lb load; add direct CPAT simulations weekly

## 8.4 Relevant Assessments
- **CPAT** — 10:20 pass; see Appendix A for full event detail
- **NFPA 1582 aerobic standard:** ≥12 METs (~42 mL/kg/min) — corresponds to 1.5-mile run ≤12:00
- **NFPA 1583** annual: aerobic, body composition, strength, endurance, flexibility
- **WFI** annual evaluation
- **Wildland Pack Test (Arduous):** 3 mi @ 45 lb in ≤45 min walking pace

## 8.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
The most common CPAT failure is **inadequate stair climb prep** — candidates use a stair-climber without weight or only do treadmill incline walking. **Coaching cue: replicate the actual load and cadence** — 50-lb vest plus 25 lb on shoulders, 60 steps/min, 3 minutes. Most commercial gyms have a StepMill; if not, rent or buy one. Second pitfall: **gripping the rails on stair climb** — disqualifying. Train hands-off from the start. Third: **inadequate posterior chain** — hose drag and ceiling pull are RDL/row patterns under load; if the candidate cannot deadlift 1.25× bodyweight, train it first.

> **Health screen:** NFPA 1582 Category A medical conditions disqualify candidates regardless of fitness. Aerobic capacity <12 METs flagged in updated 2022 standard. Always confirm medical clearance before CPAT prep.

## 8.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for severely deconditioned candidates:** start with 6 weeks of Module 1 base building before entering CPAT-specific prep.
- **Progression post-CPAT:** transition to NFPA 1583 / WFI in-service maintenance — periodized aerobic + strength + chassis program with annual re-test.

# MODULE 9 — POLICE ACADEMY / FBI PFT PREPARATION

## 9.1 Population & Goal
LE candidate 8–16 weeks out from academy entry test, in-academy fitness test, FBI Special Agent PFT, or state POST exam (CA WSTB, FL FDLE PAT, NY State Trooper PT, NYPD JST, Texas DPS PRT). Goal: pass entry standard with margin and reach graduation standard (typically 50th–70th percentile Cooper).

## 9.2 Programming Principles
- **FBI PFT (post-2025 changes):** 4 events — pull-ups (now graded mandatory), 300m sprint, push-ups, 1.5-mile run. Sit-ups removed. Pass = ≥10 points total, ≥1 in each event. Pass <10 / -2 in any event = fail.
- **State POST tests vary** — California WSTB requires 384 points across 99-yd obstacle course, 165-lb body drag, 6-ft fence climb, 6-ft solid wall, 500-yd sprint. FL FDLE PAT is a 6:04 obstacle/dummy-drag/dry-fire course. NY State Trooper PT uses Cooper percentile cuts.
- **Cooper Institute LE percentile norms** are the dominant reference framework: 40th percentile (entry), 50th percentile (graduation), 70th percentile (incumbent in-service).
- **Sprint power and short-distance speed** matter: 300m sprint scoring drops sharply if pace is off; obstacle/wall events are explosive.
- **Body drag / dummy drag** strength: train deadlift, sled drag, farmer carry, and prone-to-stand.

## 9.3 Sample 12-Week FBI PFT / POST Template

**Schedule: 5 days/week.**

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | Pull-up density (5 × 60% max, 90 sec rest) + push-up density (10 × 50% max, 60 sec rest) + 1-mile easy run |
| Tue | Sprint work: 300m sprint at goal pace, 5 min rest, 4 reps; vertical jump + broad jump practice |
| Wed | Strength: deadlift 5×5 + sled drag (165-lb sim) 4×100 ft + farmer carry |
| Thu | Run intervals: 6 × 800m @ goal 1.5-mi pace; recovery jog |
| Fri | Obstacle/wall practice (6-ft wall climb-overs, fence climbs); push-up + sit-up max test (every 2 weeks) |
| Sat | Long run 4–5 mi @ Z2; flexibility (sit-and-reach) |
| Sun | Off |

**Pull-up specific progression (FBI PFT):** to score 10 points, target male 16+ / female 8+ reps (interpolated). Use **StrongFirst ROP-style ladders** (1,2,3,4,5 × 5 ladders) 3×/week until 10-rep base; then weighted pull-up 5×3 for top-end strength.

**300m sprint progression:** target male ≤49.9 sec / female ≤49.9 sec for 10 points. Intervals at 200m (faster pace), 300m (race pace), 400m (over-distance). 4 × 300m @ goal pace, 5 min rest.

## 9.4 Relevant Assessments
See Appendix A for FBI PFT scoring, CA POST WSTB events and 384-point standard, FL FDLE PAT 6:04 standard, NY State Trooper Cooper percentile cuts, Cooper LE norms, NTOA SWAT PFQ, DEA PTA scoring, USSS PEA prerequisites.

## 9.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
Most common LE-prep failure is **insufficient pull-up training** for FBI PFT — under the 2025 changes, pull-ups are mandatory and graded; candidates who couldn't do pull-ups under the 2024 system can no longer skip it. **Coaching cue: pull-ups are a daily practice (GTG) for 8+ weeks before the test.** Second: **300m sprint pacing** — most candidates run it like a 100m sprint and crash at 200m. Train negative splits and even pace. Third: **body composition** — academies often have BCA standards; some candidates pass fitness but fail body fat percentage.

For state POST tests with obstacle / wall / drag events: practice the **specific events** — 6-ft fence climb (jump, lock arms over top, swing legs over) and 165-lb dummy drag (low center of gravity, walking backward, drive through legs). These are skills, not just fitness.

## 9.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for older candidate (35+):** allow longer prep window (16–20 weeks); emphasize joint-friendly variants (trap bar deadlift, neutral-grip pull-up, walking instead of running for some Z2 work).
- **Progression to academy / Module 10 (SWAT prep):** post-academy graduation, transition to Module 2 Operator Maintenance; if pursuing SWAT, drop into Module 10 6 months before selection.

# MODULE 10 — SWAT/HRT-STYLE TACTICAL OPERATOR

## 10.1 Population & Goal
Active LE patrol officer / federal agent **6–12 months from a SWAT, HRT, FBI Field SWAT, USSS CAT, ATF SRT, DEA SRT, or US Marshals SOG selection**. Already passed academy and PFT. Goal: build the **strength, work capacity, ruck, swim, and load-bearing tolerance** needed for selection courses that mix tactical-team physical demands (heavy gear, dynamic entry, casualty drag, climbs) with ruck/swim events.

## 10.2 Programming Principles
- **Many selection cuts are not publicly published.** HRT, CAT, SRT, SOG, LAPD D-Platoon — the agencies do not publish numerical cuts. Coaching sources (Mountain Tactical, Building the Elite, Stew Smith) interpolate from operator interviews; treat these as approximations, not policy.
- **NTOA SWAT PFQ** is the most widely-used voluntary national SWAT standard — 5 AMRAP-style events scored to 50 points; tested in slick uniform/boots.
- **MTI publishes specific selection plans** for FBI HRT, FBI SWAT, USMS SOG, FBI Academy — 7–9 weeks each, 6 days/week. These are the most-validated public templates.
- **Strength priority** with high-volume work capacity. TB Black Protocol (Operator + 2–3 HIC + occasional E) is a strong continuation framework.
- **Swim**: HRT, CAT, and several SWAT teams require water competency — 500m continuous swim + 10-minute water tread (feet only) is a commonly cited standard.
- **Ruck**: HRT-prep coaches cite 12+ miles at ~14–18 min/mile with 45–65 lb load.

## 10.3 Sample 12-Week Template (HRT-style selection prep)

**Schedule: 6 days/week.**

| Day | Session |
|-----|---------|
| Mon | TB Operator strength (Squat / Bench / WPU) — 6-week wave |
| Tue | Work capacity: Operator Ugly-style mock (max bench @ 185 / max FS @ 185 / 60s max DL @ 225 / 4×60-on/60-off shuttles / max pull-ups / 10-min SBGU AMRAP @ 60 lb) — full mock every 4 weeks; component training other weeks |
| Wed | Swim: 500m continuous + 10-min tread + 4×50m underwater |
| Thu | TB Operator strength (Bench / DL / WPU) |
| Fri | Kit run: 25-lb vest 3-mile run + sled drag 165-lb + casualty drag simulation + stair climb in kit |
| Sat | Long ruck 8–14 mi @ 45–65 lb, 14–16 min/mile |
| Sun | Off (active recovery / mobility) |

**Operator Ugly mock test specifics (MTI):**
- Max-rep bench @ 185 lb (warmup 10×135, 5×165)
- Max-rep front squat @ 185 lb
- Max-rep hinge lift in 60 sec @ 225 lb
- 4 rounds × 60-sec / 60-sec rest of 25m shuttles with prone drops
- Max strict pull-ups
- Max SBGU in 10 min @ 60 lb
- 3-mile run for time in 25-lb vest (≤30 min pass/fail)
- **Score:** Bench + FS + Hinge + Sprints + Pull-ups + (SBGU/2). Min passing = 100. Tactical goal 140+ (160–200 lb bodyweight bracket).

## 10.4 Relevant Assessments
See Appendix A for FBI HRT (publicly approximated), USSS CAT (PEA Excellent + reported sub-9:00 1.5-mi + 3 weighted pull-ups), DEA PTA (12 points pass), USMS SOG (FIT test + reported 5-mile ruck @ 45 lb in cammies), CA POST WSTB, NTOA SWAT PFQ.

## 10.5 Common Pitfalls & Coaching Cues
Most common SWAT/HRT-prep failure is **underestimating swim and ruck**. LE candidates from primarily-strength backgrounds often arrive without sustained swim or ruck capacity. **Coaching cue: water and load-bearing tolerance must be trained for 6+ months minimum.** Second: **shoulder injury from over-volumed pull-ups + bench** — use ROP-style pull-up ladders and antagonist ratio (every 2 push reps = 1 pull rep). Third: **kit-run anaerobic capacity** — selection events are short and explosive in kit; train in actual kit (or 25-lb vest), do shuttle work and stair sprints.

> **Methodology note:** For agencies with undisclosed cuts (HRT, CAT, SRT, SOG), prepare to the **publicly-known operator-tier baselines**: Operator Ugly score 140+, BUD/S-equivalent PST competitive (500-yd swim ≤9:00, push-ups 90+, sit-ups 90+, pull-ups 18+, 1.5-mi run ≤9:30), 12-mile ruck @ 65 lb in <2:45. If you can hit these, you can survive any major LE tactical selection physically.

## 10.6 Progressions / Regressions
- **Regression for in-service patrol shift constraints:** drop to MTI's "LE Patrol Athlete" plans (Spirits series — Whiskey, Tequila — 4 days/week, 45 min, balanced); pick up to 6×/week 12 weeks before selection.
- **Progression to selected operator in-service:** post-selection, drop into Module 2 (Operator Maintenance) with TB Black Protocol.

## CRITICAL CAVEATS FOR GUNNY (READ BEFORE COACHING)

**Currency:** Many standards changed in late 2025 / early 2026. Cross-check before relying on numbers older than 12 months: AFT replaced ACFT (Jun 2025); Marine combat-arms sex-neutral (Jan 2026); FBI PFT pull-ups graded (2025); Air Force PFRA new charts (Feb 2026); Coast Guard PRP rolling out 2026.

**Confidential cuts:** HRT, CAT, SRT, SOG, LAPD D-Platoon, ATF SRT, BUD/S internal phase cuts, MARSOC A&S internal cuts, SFAS internal cuts — agencies do not publish numerical cuts. Any single number cited online for these is interpolated coaching-source approximation. Train to publicly-known operator-tier baselines (BUD/S competitive PST, Ranger 12-mi <2:30, ACFT 500, sub-9:00 1.5-mi) as proxies.

**Methodology disagreements summary** (covered in detail in Run 2 appendix):
- **MTI vs. TB on conditioning:** MTI = concurrent multi-modal Fluid Periodization year-round. TB = phase-based (Base Building → Black or Green protocol with HIC/E classification).
- **MTI vs. TB on strength loading:** MTI = 3RM + absolute pound progressions (Big 24, density). TB = TM (90% 1RM) + percentage waves.
- **CrossFit vs. periodized strength:** CrossFit = constantly varied, no explicit periodization. MTI/TB/NSCA TSAC = periodization mandatory; injury epidemiology mixed (CF rate comparable to other sports per recent reviews, but tactical critics cite lack of strength predictability).
- **StrongFirst (anti-glycolytic) vs. CrossFit (glycolytic metcons):** AGT short power bursts + full recovery vs. accumulation of lactate. Different physiological adaptations.
- **NSW PTG volume caps vs. high-volume calisthenics culture:** NSW explicitly recommends 200/200/50 daily and 1000/1000/250 weekly caps for push-ups/sit-ups/pull-ups; many tactical-prep cultures exceed these and accumulate overuse injuries.
