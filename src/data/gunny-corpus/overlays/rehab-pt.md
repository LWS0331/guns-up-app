## <a id="_j23i4c89z8nn"></a>__A Full\-Spectrum PT Knowledge Base for the Guns Up Drill Instructor AI Character__

## <a id="_54635ekdixd2"></a>__0\. HOW TO USE THIS CORPUS__

This is a reference document, not a clinical decision tool\. Gunny's job is to be the noisy, no\-bullshit middle layer between a user's gym injury and the medical professional who must ultimately diagnose and clear them\. The corpus is organized so Gunny can:

1. Recognize what a user is describing\.
2. Triage it \(self\-manageable / needs PT or MD / emergency referral\)\.
3. Educate on mechanism, healing biology, and timeline\.
4. Prescribe phased, evidence\-graded loading and mobility work\.
5. Coach mindset \(pain monitoring, kinesiophobia, sleep, nutrition\)\.

__Evidence grading used throughout:__

- __STRONG__ — Multiple high\-quality RCTs and systematic reviews \(JOSPT, BJSM, Cochrane\)\.
- __MODERATE__ — Consistent expert consensus \+ supporting RCTs\.
- __EMERGING__ — Promising mechanism \+ limited human trials\.
- __ANECDOTAL/PRACTITIONER__ — Coached and used widely; weak or mixed RCT data\.

__Mandatory Medical Referral Triggers \(Gunny defers — no exceptions\):__

- Loss of bowel/bladder control, saddle anesthesia, bilateral leg weakness/numbness \(cauda equina — ER\)\.
- Acute trauma with deformity, inability to bear weight, audible "pop" with immediate swelling and instability\.
- Night pain, unexplained weight loss, fever with back pain, history of cancer\.
- Progressive neurological deficit \(worsening weakness, foot drop\)\.
- Chest pain, calf pain with swelling/redness \(DVT/PE\)\.
- Open wounds, suspected fracture, dislocation\.
- Pain unresponsive to 4–6 weeks of conservative loading\.

## <a id="_hq8lqj5pcfab"></a>__1\. FOUNDATIONAL PT KNOWLEDGE__

### <a id="_v1f5m4tgnpkb"></a>__1\.1 Tissue Healing Timelines__

Tissue healing proceeds through four overlapping phases \(Healogics; *Skin Acute Wound Healing* PMC6582859; Wikipedia "Wound healing"\)\. Although wound\-healing literature is dermatological, the same biology applies to muscle, tendon, ligament, and post\-op tissue with longer durations:

__Loading principle__

__Tissue\-specific approximate timelines:__

- Muscle strain: 2–6 weeks \(Grade I\), 6–12 weeks \(Grade II\), 3\+ months \(Grade III\)\.
- Ligament sprain: 6–12 weeks \(Grade I–II\); ACL/major ligament rupture often requires surgical timeline of 9–12\+ months\.
- Tendon: minimum __8–12 weeks__ for symptom change; structural remodeling takes __months to a year__ under load \(Cook/Purdam\)\.
- Bone \(stress fracture\): __6–8 weeks__ for low\-risk sites; __12–21 weeks__ for high\-risk femoral/tibial; metatarsals ~12 weeks \(Wood et al\., RM training study\)\.
- Cartilage: poor blood supply → very slow; meniscus tears sometimes never structurally heal but become asymptomatic\.

__Coaching point:__ Tissue heals on biology's clock, not the user's\. Pushing through the proliferation phase often resets it\.

### <a id="_c1twoal3x6bj"></a>__1\.2 Pain Science Basics__

- __Acute pain__ is a protective alarm tied to actual or potential tissue damage\. It generally tracks with healing\.
- __Chronic pain__ \(>3 months\) often outlives the original injury and is driven by changes in the nervous system itself, not ongoing tissue damage \(Meeus & Nijs 2007; IASP/Institute for Chronic Pain\)\.
- __Central sensitization__ = the spinal cord and brain amplify normal signals \(allodynia: pain to non\-painful stimuli; hyperalgesia: exaggerated pain\)\. Documented in fibromyalgia, chronic low back pain, post\-surgical pain syndromes\.
- __Biopsychosocial model:__ Pain = nociceptive input × beliefs × stress × sleep × context\. Two people with identical MRIs can have vastly different pain\.
- __Hurt ≠ Harm:__ A core teaching point\. Pain is information, not always damage\.

### <a id="_gt3zrzkz625z"></a>__1\.3 Load Management — Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio \(ACWR\)__

Tim Gabbett's framework \(BJSM 2016; Andrade et al\. 2020 systematic review\):

- __Chronic load__ = rolling 4\-week average of training load\.
- __Acute load__ = current week's load\.
- __ACWR sweet spot ≈ 0\.8–1\.3\.__ Above ~1\.5, injury risk rises 2–5x in rugby and Australian football data; below ~0\.8 may indicate detraining\.
- Caveat: Impellizzeri \(2020\) and others have challenged ACWR as a predictive model\. Best treated as a __monitoring heuristic, not a prediction__\. The "10% rule" for runners is a similar field\-expedient tool\.

__Practical Gunny rule:__ Don't add more than ~10% per week to running mileage, ruck weight, or working sets\. After a layoff, restart at 50–60% of prior load\.

### <a id="_3tnszityvdku"></a>__1\.4 Tendon Rehab Principles \(Continuum: Isometric → Eccentric → Heavy Slow Resistance\)__

Based on Cook & Purdam continuum, Alfredson \(1998\), Silbernagel \(2007\), Beyer/Kongsgaard HSR work, and Malliaras et al\. JOSPT 2015\.

__Stage 1 — Isometric loading for analgesia & early load tolerance:__

- 5 sets × 30–45 seconds at ~70% MVC, 1–2 min rest, 1–2x/day\.
- Rio et al\. demonstrated immediate analgesia and reduced cortical inhibition in patellar tendinopathy\.
- Short\-duration isometrics \(e\.g\., 5×10s\) work too if total time\-under\-tension is matched\.

__Stage 2 — Isotonic \(heavy slow resistance / eccentric\):__

- *Alfredson protocol* \(gold standard for mid\-portion Achilles tendinopathy\): 3 sets × 15 reps eccentric heel drops, knee straight \+ knee bent, __twice daily, 7 days/week, 12 weeks__\. Add load via backpack as tolerated\. Note: a "do\-as\-tolerated" lower\-volume version \(Stevens & Tan 2014\) achieves equivalent VISA\-A outcomes\.
- *Heavy Slow Resistance \(HSR\)* \(Beyer 2015; Kongsgaard 2009\): 3–4 sets × 6–15 reps, 3 sec concentric / 3 sec eccentric, __3x/week, 12 weeks__\. Equivalent or superior patient satisfaction vs\. Alfredson at 12 weeks\. Used for patellar, Achilles, gluteal tendinopathies\.
- *Silbernagel combined protocol*: concentric \+ eccentric \+ plyometric 4–5 exercises, 2–3 sets × 10–20 reps, daily for 12 weeks\. Equivalent to Alfredson at 1\-year follow\-up \(Habets 2021 RCT\)\.

__Stage 3 — Energy storage/release \(plyometric\):__

- Once pain ≤3/10 and load tolerance restored\. Hops, jumps, bounding\.

__Stage 4 — Return to sport:__ Sport\-specific load\.

__Silbernagel Pain\-Monitoring Model \(KEY Gunny tool\):__

- Pain during activity ≤ 5/10 is acceptable\.
- Pain must not be worse the morning after\.
- Pain must not progressively worsen week\-to\-week\.
- If those three are met, __continue training through tendinopathy__; rest is not required and often counterproductive\.

### <a id="_et8tkor4iyw6"></a>__1\.5 Mobility vs\. Flexibility vs\. Stability__

- __Flexibility__ = passive muscle length \(sit\-and\-reach\)\.
- __Mobility__ = active, controlled range of motion of a joint with strength through that range\.
- __Stability__ = ability to control motion under load — essentially strength \+ motor control at end ranges\.

Kelly Starrett's framework \(*Becoming a Supple Leopard*, MobilityWOD/Ready State\): mobility restriction is rarely just "tight muscle" — it is fascia, joint capsule, and motor control combined\. His protocol uses __compression__ \(lacrosse ball, foam roller — restore tissue sliding surfaces\), __distraction__ \(banded joint mobs — change capsular position\), and __active end\-range work__ \(load the new range so the brain accepts it\)\. His "23:59 rule": one minute of stretching can't undo 23h59m of bad postural input — change behavior, not just stretch more\.

The __Squat University \(Aaron Horschig\)__ approach borrows the "joint\-by\-joint" model \(originally Boyle/Cook\): from the ground up, joints alternate between needing mobility and stability — ankle \(mobility\), knee \(stability\), hip \(mobility\), lumbar \(stability\), thoracic \(mobility\), scapulae \(stability\), glenohumeral \(mobility\)\. When a mobility joint is stiff, the adjacent stability joint over\-moves and gets injured\.

### <a id="_p606wml4ttsq"></a>__1\.7 Sleep, Nutrition, Stress__

- __Sleep:__ <7 hr correlates with 1\.7x injury risk in adolescent athletes \(Milewski et al\.\)\. Athletes should target 8–10 hr; nap 20–90 min for sleep extension\. Consistent schedule > duration\.
- __Protein:__ 1\.6–2\.2 g/kg/day for hypertrophy and tissue repair \(ACSM/ISSN\)\. Distribute across 4–5 meals \(~0\.4 g/kg/meal\)\.
- __Collagen \+ Vitamin C:__ 15 g hydrolyzed collagen \+ 50 mg Vit C ~30–60 min pre\-loading exercise may augment tendon collagen synthesis \(Shaw 2017 mechanism study\)\.
- __Vitamin D:__ Maintain 30–50 ng/mL; deficiency increases stress fracture risk\.
- __Calcium:__ 1,000–1,500 mg/day — military stress fracture data supports adequacy\.
- __Stress:__ Chronic stress → elevated cortisol → impaired healing, sleep disruption, immune suppression\. Same mechanism as Operator Syndrome's allostatic load\.

### <a id="_z6578g3mrtlz"></a>__1\.8 Psychological Aspects of Injury Recovery__

- __Kinesiophobia__ \(Tampa Scale of Kinesiophobia, TSK; ≥37 = high\)\. High kinesiophobia predicts worse outcomes after ACL, shoulder, and hand surgeries; affects 5–10% of pain variance and 7%\+ of functional outcome variance\.
- __Fear\-avoidance model__ \(Vlaeyen, Lethem\): pain → catastrophizing → avoidance → deconditioning → more pain\.
- __Antidotes that work__ \(graded exposure literature\):
	- Education \("hurt ≠ harm" pain neuroscience education\)\.
	- Graded exposure to feared movements\.
	- Successful experience with appropriately scaled task \("win" reps\)\.
	- Self\-efficacy/confidence building\.
- For Gunny: high\-trust DI energy that reframes pain \("That's tissue talking, not breaking — earn another rep"\) matches the evidence\-based "reassurance \+ exposure" model when the pain is actually safe pain\. Critically, Gunny must distinguish __provocation pain__ \(acceptable, e\.g\., Silbernagel 5/10\) from __warning pain__ \(sharp, radiating, asymmetric\) and red\-flag symptoms\.

## <a id="_yvnlvsmvyeuy"></a>__2\. ACUTE INJURY MANAGEMENT — PEACE & LOVE__

Replaces RICE/POLICE for soft\-tissue injuries \(Dubois & Esculier, *BJSM* 2019\)\.

__PEACE — first 1–3 days:__

- __P__ rotect: Unload to avoid bleeding/distension\. Crutches/sling/brace as needed for 1–3 days only\.
- __E__ levate above heart to reduce edema\.
- __A__ void anti\-inflammatories \(NSAIDs and ice may impair tissue healing in soft tissue & tendon injuries — inflammation is necessary for repair\)\.
- __C__ ompress: Tape/bandage limits intra\-articular edema and hematoma\.
- __E__ ducate: Active over passive; avoid catastrophizing\.

__LOVE — after first few days:__

- __L__ oad: Mechanotherapy\. Pain\-adapted progressive loading promotes repair \(Khan & Scott 2009\)\.
- __O__ ptimism: Patient expectations correlate with outcomes\.
- __V__ ascularization: Pain\-free aerobic activity \(walk, bike\) drives blood flow to injury\.
- __E__ xercise: Restore mobility, strength, proprioception\.

__Caveats:__

- For moderate/severe injuries with significant swelling, the __CARE__ modification \(Fousekis & Tsepis 2021\) reintroduces brief cryotherapy \+ compression to manage oedema\-induced arthrogenic inhibition\. In practice: short cold sessions \(10–15 min\) for swelling control are still defensible; avoid using ice as anti\-inflammatory dogma\.
- __Bone, complete ligament rupture, suspected fracture__ → image first, not PEACE & LOVE\.

## <a id="_eooyp9rt4h1l"></a>__3\. MOVEMENT SCREENS & SELF\-ASSESSMENT__

### <a id="_n8w0aqr8wv2b"></a>__3\.1 Functional Movement Screen \(FMS\) — Cook/Burton__

7 patterns scored 0–3 \(0 = pain, 1 = fail, 2 = compensation, 3 = clean\), composite out of 21:

1. __Deep Squat__ \(with dowel overhead\) — global mobility/stability\.
2. __Hurdle Step__ — single\-leg stance and step mechanics\.
3. __In\-Line Lunge__ — split\-stance trunk and hip control\.
4. __Shoulder Mobility__ — combined ER\+abduction / IR\+adduction; __clearing test for impingement__: pain on hand\-behind\-head reach = score 0\.
5. __Active Straight\-Leg Raise__ — hamstring length \+ opposite hip extension\.
6. __Trunk Stability Push\-Up__ — core anti\-extension\.
7. __Rotary Stability__ — quadruped same\-side reach\.

Composite ≤14 has shown weak association with injury risk; the FMS is best used for __identifying asymmetry and pain\-provoking patterns__, not predicting injury per Moran et al\. meta\-analysis\. Asymmetry > composite score\.

### <a id="_nb9yannaxsy3"></a>__3\.2 Selective Functional Movement Assessment \(SFMA\) basics__

Top\-tier breakouts: cervical, upper extremity, multi\-segmental flexion/extension/rotation, single\-leg stance, overhead deep squat\. Each pattern → "Functional Non\-Painful," "Functional Painful," "Dysfunctional Non\-Painful," or "Dysfunctional Painful\." Pain in a pattern = caution\. Dysfunction without pain = mobility/stability problem to address\.

### <a id="_rlcxv6xlocib"></a>__3\.3 Gunny's Quick Self\-Screen \(5 movements user can do solo\)__

1. Bodyweight squat to depth — heels down, dowel overhead \(ankle/hip/T\-spine/shoulder\)\.
2. Single\-leg balance, eyes closed, 30 sec each side \(ankle proprioception, glute med\)\.
3. Wall angel against flat wall \(T\-spine extension, scapular control\)\.
4. Active straight\-leg raise, supine \(post chain\)\.
5. Hands\-and\-knees birddog hold, 10 sec/side without hip rotation \(core stability\)\.

Asymmetry > 2x or pain on any pattern → don't load that pattern, address it first\.

## <a id="_vr24hnl1r37f"></a>__4\. INJURY\-BY\-INJURY BREAKDOWN__

For each: __Anatomy/Mechanism → Causes → Signs/Red Flags → Acute Management → Phased Rehab → Specific Exercises → Mistakes → Timeline → Return\-to\-Training Criteria → Prevention\.__

### <a id="_ydoaxb2ao34c"></a>__4\.1 SHOULDER__

#### <a id="_dl8xxteio44s"></a>__4\.1\.1 Rotator Cuff Tendinopathy / Subacromial Pain Syndrome \(formerly "impingement"\)__

- __Anatomy:__ Supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis\. Subacromial bursa\. Coracoacromial arch\.
- __Mechanism:__ Repetitive overhead reach with poor scapular upward rotation; bench\-press dominance with weak external rotators; ruck\-strap compression in tactical populations\.
- __Signs:__ Painful arc 60–120°; pain reaching behind back; night pain on involved side; weakness with empty\-can / Jobe's test\.
- __Red flags:__ Sudden weakness after trauma \+ inability to lift arm = full\-thickness tear → ortho\. Constant night pain \+ weight loss → image\.
- __Acute \(first 1–2 weeks\):__ Avoid provocative overhead reps; isometrics for cuff \(ER/IR at 0° abduction, 30 sec × 5\)\.
- __Subacute/Strength \(weeks 2–8\):__ Scapular stabilization \(serratus punches, lower\-trap Y/T/W's, prone "I"s\); cuff strengthening \(sidelying ER, prone horizontal abduction\); thoracic mobility \(foam roller extensions, open books\)\.
- __Specific exercises \(Reinold/Cressey style\):__
	- Sidelying ER, 3×15, 1–5 lb\.
	- Prone Y/T/W/I, 3×10–12\.
	- Wall slides with band \(serratus\)\.
	- 1\-arm landmine press \(free\-scapula pressing\)\.
	- Face pulls 3×12–15\.
	- Bottoms\-up KB carry/press for cuff co\-contraction\.
- __Evidence:__ Scapular stabilization exercises reduce pain and disability in SAPS \(PMC10940535 meta\-analysis\)\. Eccentric cuff work has small additional benefit \(PMC6792214\)\. Surprisingly, handgrip strengthening also improves cuff pain/function \(PMC9448609\)\.
- __Mistakes:__ Going straight to overhead pressing; ignoring posterior shoulder/T\-spine mobility; over\-foam\-rolling the front delt while ignoring the cause\.
- __Timeline:__ 6–12 weeks for substantial recovery with consistent loading\. Some chronicity to 6 months\.
- __Return\-to\-training:__ Full pain\-free overhead range; ER strength within 90% of contralateral side; able to do 5 strict push\-ups without pain\.
- __Prevention:__ 2:1 pulling\-to\-pressing ratio; horizontal pulls > vertical pulls for desk\-bound users; daily 5\-min "shoulder care" with band ER/IR \+ face pulls\.

#### <a id="_415snk8t37kj"></a>__4\.1\.2 Rotator Cuff Tear \(Partial / Full Thickness\)__

- __Conservative trial first__ for partial and small full\-thickness tears in non\-laborers \(AAOS CPG\): structured rehab for 12 weeks\. Surgery for failed conservative care or large/massive tears in active patients\.
- Refer to 4\.7\.1 for post\-op protocol\.

#### <a id="_50rr21yflb84"></a>__4\.1\.3 Shoulder Impingement / SAPS Programming__

Same as 4\.1\.1 — terminology shift only\. Diercks 2014 introduced "subacromial pain syndrome" as a more accurate descriptor; "impingement" implies a specific mechanical cause that often isn't there\.

#### <a id="_dtlotg25e39"></a>__4\.1\.4 Labral Tears / SLAP Lesions__

- __Anatomy:__ Superior labrum from anterior to posterior, biceps anchor\.
- __Mechanism:__ Throwers \(peel\-back\), fall on outstretched arm \(compression\), repeated bench\-press in lifters\.
- __Signs:__ Deep clicking, pain at end\-range external rotation 90/90, O'Brien's test \+\.
- Conservative for Type I and many Type II; surgery for Type III/IV\.
- Post\-op see 4\.7\.5\.

#### <a id="_hvkkskg1qeev"></a>__4\.1\.5 AC Joint Sprain \(Shoulder Separation\)__

- __Mechanism:__ Fall on point of shoulder\.
- __Rockwood grades I–VI:__ I–II treated nonoperatively; III variable \(most heal conservatively in 6–12 weeks\); IV–VI typically surgical\.
- __Acute:__ Sling 1–2 weeks \(Grade II–III\), ice, NSAIDs short course\.
- __Phase 1 \(weeks 0–2\):__ Sling, pendulums, AROM elbow/wrist, shoulder isometrics, scapular setting\.
- __Phase 2 \(weeks 2–6\):__ Range\-of\-motion restoration \(avoid horizontal adduction early as it loads AC\); rotator cuff isometrics → light isotonic; lower trap \(prone "I"\); resisted rows palms\-up\.
- __Phase 3 \(weeks 6–12\):__ Pressing/overhead returns; load progression\.
- __Avoid:__ Direct dips, heavy bench, overhead pressing until pain\-free\.
- __Return:__ Full ROM, no pain on cross\-body adduction, 90% strength symmetry\.

#### <a id="_700o61hdu1cx"></a>__4\.1\.6 Anterior Shoulder Instability / Bankart__

- After dislocation: 3–6 weeks of immobilization debated; modern data favors early progressive ROM with avoidance of provocative ER\+abduction\. Conservative or surgical \(Bankart/Latarjet\) based on age, sport demand, recurrence rate\.

### <a id="_bjri7li0uo59"></a>__4\.2 ELBOW__

#### <a id="_6xl67mvgc8et"></a>__4\.2\.1 Lateral Epicondylopathy \("Tennis Elbow"\)__

- __Anatomy:__ Common extensor tendon at lateral epicondyle \(ECRB primarily\)\.
- __Mechanism:__ Repetitive wrist extension/gripping; rower, painter, plumber, climber, lifter pulling tasks\.
- __Tyler Twist \(FlexBar eccentric protocol\):__ Tyler et al\., evidence\-supported\.
	- 3 sets × 15 reps daily\.
	- Each rep: 4 seconds, twist with healthy hand to load extension; release with affected hand controlling eccentric\.
	- 6\+ weeks\. Effective in multiple RCTs \(PMC2971639; Cureus 2023 RCT vs\. ART\)\.
- __Adjunct:__ Heavy slow grip strengthening, forearm rolls, cross\-friction massage\.
- __Timeline:__ 6–12 weeks\.
- __Avoid:__ Pure rest \(deconditioning\); cortisone injections \(worse long\-term outcomes vs\. exercise per multiple RCTs\)\.

#### <a id="_96geshgkldcf"></a>__4\.2\.2 Medial Epicondylopathy \("Golfer's Elbow"\)__

- Common flexor tendon\. __Reverse Tyler Twist__ \(FlexBar\) — same protocol, opposite direction \(eccentric for flexors/pronators\)\.

#### <a id="_yu4i4h5dftov"></a>__4\.2\.3 Triceps Tendinopathy / Distal Triceps Tear__

- Tendinopathy: Heavy slow resistance triceps extension \(skull crusher 4×6–8, 3 sec eccentric\)\.
- Acute triceps rupture \(rare but notable in heavy lifters/anabolic users\): surgical\.

#### <a id="_64g1b6232bf5"></a>__4\.2\.4 Ulnar Nerve / Cubital Tunnel__

- Avoid prolonged elbow flexion, sleep with elbow brace to prevent flexion at night, ulnar nerve glides 2x/day\.

### <a id="_jvqn3s6bn265"></a>__4\.3 WRIST / HAND__

#### <a id="_s47xeznycljk"></a>__4\.3\.1 Carpal Tunnel Syndrome__

- __Mechanism:__ Median nerve compression in carpal tunnel\.
- __Signs:__ Nocturnal numbness/tingling thumb\-index\-middle\-half ring; thenar atrophy in advanced\.
- __Conservative \(mild–moderate\):__
	- Neutral wrist splint at night \(most evidence\-supported single intervention\)\.
	- Tendon and median nerve gliding exercises 5x/day \(Rozmaryn\)\.
	- Ergonomic modification\.
	- Ultrasound therapy adjunctive\.
- Severe / thenar atrophy / failed conservative → surgical release\.

#### <a id="_su47wdmdes4g"></a>__4\.3\.2 Wrist Sprains / TFCC__

- Acute: brace 1–2 weeks, gentle AROM, then progressive grip/wrist strengthening\. Persistent ulnar\-side pain → image \(TFCC\)\.

### <a id="_2inoyyfxw4i1"></a>__4\.4 LOW BACK / SPINE__

#### <a id="_gmetsv8x7cop"></a>__4\.4\.1 Non\-Specific Low Back Pain \(>90% of LBP\)__

- __Modern model:__ Often nociplastic \+ biomechanical \+ biopsychosocial\. Avoid "your spine is broken" narratives\.
- __Stuart McGill Big 3__ \(STRONG evidence for endurance\-focused stabilization\):
	- __Modified Curl\-Up__ — supine, one knee bent, hands under lumbar lordosis, lift head/shoulders as a unit\. 8–10 sec hold\. Reverse pyramid: 6, 4, 2 reps\.
	- __Side Bridge / Side Plank__ — knees bent for regression\. 10 sec hold, 6 reps each side\.
	- __Bird Dog__ — opposite arm/leg, 10 sec hold, descending pyramid\.
- Cat\-camel as warm\-up \(5–6 cycles\) — mobility WITHOUT loaded flexion\.
- McGill principle: __endurance over max strength__ for the spine; spare repetitive loaded flexion \(avoid sit\-ups, weighted Russian twists\) early in rehab\.

#### <a id="_vnctjlswp468"></a>__4\.4\.2 Disc Herniation / Radiculopathy__

- __Acute \(0–6 weeks\):__ McKenzie repeated extension if it centralizes pain \(pain moves out of leg toward back = good sign\)\. If extension peripheralizes \(worsens leg pain\), use flexion\-bias or directional preference per assessment\.
- Nerve flossing/sciatic nerve glides 2x/day \(slider technique tends to be better tolerated than tensioner for irritable nerves\)\.
- Walking 2x/day, 10–20 min\.
- Avoid prolonged sitting > 30 min, deadlifts, loaded flexion\.
- __Subacute → strength:__ McGill Big 3 daily\. Hip hinge mechanics\. Glute strengthening \(hip thrusts, bridges\)\.
- __Evidence:__ Exercise therapy reduces VAS, ODI, improves QOL in LDH \(PMC11985520 meta\-analysis\)\. McKenzie centralizes pain\.
- __Conservative trial:__ 6–12 weeks before considering surgery in absence of red flags\. ~85–90% improve without surgery\.
- __Red flags → ER:__ Cauda equina \(saddle anesthesia, bowel/bladder loss, bilateral leg weakness\); progressive motor deficit\.

#### <a id="_qcz3cildgc7v"></a>__4\.4\.3 Facet Joint Pain__

- Often extension\-aggravated \(kissing spines pattern\)\. Flexion\-bias rehab: knee\-to\-chest, posterior pelvic tilts, dead bug, hip flexor stretching\.

#### <a id="_j3h19i5y5qix"></a>__4\.4\.4 SI Joint Dysfunction__

- __Mechanism:__ Often "force closure" failure — the joint is fine, surrounding muscles aren't\. Common in postpartum, runners with glute weakness\.
- __Rehab focus:__ Glute med \(clamshells, banded sidestep, side\-lying leg lift\), glute max \(bridges, hip thrusts\), TFL/QL release, controlled rotation work\. Avoid asymmetric loading early\.
- Manual therapy \+ exercise > exercise alone short\-term \(Al\-Subahi 2017 systematic review\)\.

#### <a id="_4j8lz77hy0yt"></a>__4\.4\.5 Muscle Strain \(Erector Spinae, QL\)__

- 1–3 days protect, then early movement\. Walking, gentle ROM\. Progress to McGill Big 3 by week 1–2\. Most resolve in 2–4 weeks\.

#### <a id="_q26nz4mrbted"></a>__4\.4\.6 Sciatica / Radiculopathy__

See 4\.4\.2\. Slider nerve glides \+ directional preference exercise \+ walking\. NSAIDs short course OK\. Most resolve in 6–12 weeks\.

#### <a id="_3qj42uqufhj"></a>__4\.4\.7 Thoracic Outlet Syndrome \(TOS\)__

- __Neurogenic TOS__ \(95% of cases\): Brachial plexus compression at scalenes / costoclavicular / pec minor\.
- __Signs:__ Arm heaviness/numbness with overhead use; ulnar\-sided more often; symptoms with rucking shoulder strap pressure\.
- __Conservative:__ Postural retraining, scalene/pec minor release, scapular setting \(lower trap, serratus\), nerve glides, breathing diaphragm work\. 60–70% of NTOS chooses surgery due to suboptimal conservative outcomes — evidence base is weak; persist with quality therapy first\.

### <a id="_mm5349sjul8n"></a>__4\.5 HIP__

#### <a id="_ayeco3fvnne5"></a>__4\.5\.1 Femoroacetabular Impingement \(FAI\) / Labral Tears__

- __Cam__ \(femoral neck bump\), __Pincer__ \(over\-coverage from acetabulum\), or mixed\.
- __Signs:__ Anterior groin pain \(the C\-sign\); pain in deep squat, prolonged sitting, sharp turns\. FADIR test \+\.
- __Conservative \(first\-line per recent UK trial\):__ ~44% satisfied without surgery at 1 year; both groups improved\.
	- Avoid deep squat past pain\-free range; substitute box squat, Bulgarian split, RDL\.
	- Glute med/max strengthening; core anti\-rotation \(Pallof press\)\.
	- Hip mobility \(90/90 hip rotations, world's greatest stretch — but careful of pinch\)\.
	- 12\+ weeks\.
- Surgery \(arthroscopic labral repair \+ osteoplasty\) for failures\.

#### <a id="_hm57spc98eds"></a>__4\.5\.2 Hip Flexor Strain / Tendinopathy__

- __Iliopsoas strain:__ acute groin pain on resisted hip flexion\. Acute: 2–7 days protect → isometric hip flexion supine → progressive concentric → eccentric \(Copenhagen\-style adapted\)\.
- Tendinopathy: heavy slow resistance hip flexion \(banded standing, supine plate marches\)\.

#### <a id="_etb34mfcs5m1"></a>__4\.5\.3 Adductor Strain \("Groin Pull"\)__

- __Phase 1 \(acute, 24–48h–7 days\):__ Sidelying isometric adduction with ball squeeze; pain\-free walking\.
- __Phase 2:__ Concentric adduction with band/cable; sumo squat; lateral lunge\.
- __Phase 3:__ Copenhagen adduction \(single leg sliding, advanced\)\.
- __Phase 4:__ Plyometric/sport\-specific \(skater hops, lateral shuffles, change of direction\)\.
- Adductor:abductor strength ratio 0\.95\+ to return to sport\.

#### <a id="_m50y6tbc6cjk"></a>__4\.5\.4 Gluteal Tendinopathy \(Lateral Hip Pain\)__

- Heavy slow resistance hip abduction \(clamshell loaded → side\-lying abduction with weight → standing band abduction → step\-ups\)\. Avoid prolonged crossing legs and standing on one hip \("hanging on the hip"\)\.

#### <a id="_3osr44wcb4h"></a>__4\.5\.5 Total Hip Arthroplasty \(post\-op\) — see 4\.7\.4__

### <a id="_rlpb44n2pvpp"></a>__4\.6 KNEE__

#### <a id="_sx70bueza0oh"></a>__4\.6\.1 Patellar Tendinopathy \(Jumper's Knee\)__

- __Diagnosis:__ Pain at inferior pole patella, single\-leg decline squat reproduces pain\.
- __Malliaras/Cook 4\-stage protocol \(JOSPT 2015 — STRONG evidence\):__
	- __Stage 1 — Isometrics:__ Spanish squat, leg extension hold at 60°\. 5×45 sec @ 70% MVC, 1–2 min rest\. 2x/day\. Immediate analgesia \(Rio\)\.
	- __Stage 2 — Isotonic \(HSR\):__ Heavy slow squat, leg press, leg extension\. 3–4×6–15 reps, 3 sec each direction, 3x/week\.
	- __Stage 3 — Energy storage:__ Squat jumps, depth jumps, single\-leg hops\.
	- __Stage 4 — Energy storage and release / sport\-specific\.__
- __Pain monitoring:__ Silbernagel scale ≤5/10 acceptable; must settle by next morning\.
- __Timeline:__ 12\+ weeks\. Don't stop loading at symptom resolution — structural remodeling continues\.

#### <a id="_7ggbf4lrqhrg"></a>__4\.6\.2 Meniscus Tears__

- __Degenerative tears__ \(middle\-aged, no trauma\): Conservative > surgery long\-term per multiple systematic reviews \(PMC10192166 — APM no long\-term advantage\)\. Quad/hamstring/glute strengthening, BFR useful for early loading without joint stress\.
- __Traumatic peripheral tears in young active patients:__ Repair preferred over meniscectomy; meniscectomy accelerates OA\.
- __Bucket\-handle / locked knee:__ Surgical\.

#### <a id="_un98j1ka1v66"></a>__4\.6\.3 ACL / MCL__

- __Non\-operative:__ "Copers" — pre\-op strength asymmetry <20%, no instability\. ~40% pass return\-to\-sport at 6 months\.
- __Surgical__ — see 4\.7\.2\.
- __MCL Grade I–II__ typically heals conservatively in 4–8 weeks with progressive loading and brace\.

#### <a id="_a362abhmqf32"></a>__4\.6\.4 Patellar Tendon, Meniscectomy, ACL post\-op — see Section 4\.7__

#### <a id="_dkg2nn8ib928"></a>__4\.6\.5 Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome__

- __Hip\-focused rehab__ beats knee\-focused rehab \(Earl/Hoch\)\. Glute med/max strengthening, hip ER, quad strengthening with attention to VMO, soft tissue work to lateral retinaculum\.

#### <a id="_xocsymxugl95"></a>__4\.6\.6 IT Band Syndrome__

- __Mechanism:__ Compression at lateral femoral epicondyle, NOT friction\. Caused by hip abductor weakness and femoral adduction during stance\.
- __Stop foam\-rolling the IT band__ — it's resistant to stretch \(<0\.2% MVC\); roll TFL/quad/glute instead\.
- __Rehab:__ Glute med endurance \(Fredericson protocol — 3×30 reps abduction in side\-lying, hip airplanes, single\-leg bridges\)\. 6 weeks → 90% pain\-free in original Fredericson trial\.
- Reduce running mileage 30–50% during rehab; avoid downhill running\.

#### <a id="_mpcbbpp3m15g"></a>__4\.7\.6 Lumbar Discectomy / Microdiscectomy__

- __Phase 1 \(0–2 weeks\):__ Walking, no lifting >10 lbs, sitting <30 min, no twisting/loaded flexion\.
- __Phase 2 \(2–6 weeks\):__ Begin PT — gentle core \(dead bug, bird dog\), nerve glides, walking 30\+ min\.
- __Phase 3 \(6–12 weeks\):__ Progressive lumbar stabilization \(McGill Big 3\), light deadlift mechanics with body weight then load\.
- __Phase 4 \(3–6 months\):__ Return to gym; running progression; lifting unrestricted by 6 months in most cases\.
- __Evidence:__ Early PT \(within 2 weeks post\-op\) reduces post\-op back pain and improves function \(PMC11084320\)\.

#### <a id="_groneaopk8es"></a>__4\.7\.7 Achilles Tendon Repair__

- __Modern accelerated protocol \(Mitrogiannis 2024; Yotsumoto/MSLLS technique\):__
	- Removable boot from day 1 with active ROM ankle exercises\.
	- Full WB in walking boot with foot plantigrade by week 2\.
	- Out of boot week 6–8\.
	- HSR/eccentric loading week 8–12\.
	- Plyometrics 4–5 months; return to running 6 months; full sport 9–12 months\.
- __Classic protocol:__ 6–12 weeks NWB cast in plantarflexion — older, increasingly abandoned\.

### <a id="_vymefx4bfg6p"></a>__4\.8 LOWER LEG / FOOT / ANKLE__

#### <a id="_p33w3eszblhk"></a>__4\.8\.1 Achilles Tendinopathy__

See 1\.4 \(Alfredson, HSR, Silbernagel protocols\)\. 12\-week loading mandatory\. 5\-year follow\-up \(Silbernagel 2011\): majority recover with exercise alone\.

#### <a id="_ilkwtro00kez"></a>__4\.8\.2 Plantar Fasciopathy__

- __Rathleff High\-Load Strength Training Protocol__ \(STRONG\):
	- Single\-leg heel raise on a step __with towel under toes__ to dorsiflex MTP joints \(engages windlass mechanism, loads plantar fascia\)\.
	- Weeks 1–2: 3×12 RM
	- Weeks 3–4: 4×10 RM
	- Weeks 5–8\+: 5×8 RM
	- Every other day, 3 sec concentric / 3 sec eccentric\.
	- Outperformed plantar\-specific stretching on FFI by 29 points at 3 months \(Rathleff 2015\)\.
- Adjuncts: heel cup/insole; calf/intrinsic foot strengthening; toe yoga\.

#### <a id="_e5gxs37ba8wu"></a>__4\.8\.3 Medial Tibial Stress Syndrome \(Shin Splints\)__

- __Highly prevalent in military recruits \(35–56%\)__ per scoping reviews\.
- __Risk factors:__ Female sex, high BMI, low aerobic baseline fitness, smoking, navicular drop, hip ER deficit, sudden volume increase\.
- __Acute:__ 2–6 weeks reduced/cross\-train activity; ice/compression for symptoms; address running mechanics \(cadence ↑ to 170–180, reduce overstride\)\.
- __Strengthening:__ Calf complex \(gastroc \+ soleus\), tibialis anterior/posterior, hip abductors\. Heel raises with knee bent \(soleus\) and straight \(gastroc\)\. Anti\-pronation foot intrinsic work\.
- __Modalities with evidence:__ ESWT, compression, shock\-absorbing insoles\.
- __Return\-to\-running:__ Walk\-to\-run progression after 2 weeks pain\-free on impact testing \(single\-leg hops × 10\)\.

#### <a id="_3jglxq188sfc"></a>__4\.8\.4 Lateral Ankle Sprain__

- __Acute \(PEACE 1–3 days\):__ Brace/tape, weight\-bear as tolerated, AROM \(alphabet drawing\)\.
- __Subacute \(3–14 days\):__ Progressive loading\. ROM, isometrics → resisted band eversion/inversion, calf raises\.
- __Strength/Proprioception \(2–6 weeks\):__ Single\-leg balance, BAPS/wobble board, eyes\-closed challenges, lateral hops\.
- __Return\-to\-sport \(6–12 weeks\):__ Plyometric progression, agility \(T\-drill, 5\-10\-5\)\.
- __Evidence:__ Exercise\-based rehab reduces re\-injury risk significantly \(PMC8824326 systematic review\)\. Up to 40% develop chronic ankle instability without proper rehab — single biggest mistake is early return without proprioceptive training\.
- __High ankle sprain \(syndesmotic\):__ Longer rehab \(6–12\+ weeks\); avoid external rotation stress; brace in boot 4–6 weeks\.

#### <a id="_c5cpccae9nkq"></a>__4\.8\.5 Stress Fractures__

- __Low\-risk__ \(posteromedial tibia, fibula, metatarsal shafts 2–4\): Non\-WB → WB transition, 4–8 weeks rest from impact\.
- __High\-risk__ \(anterior tibia "dreaded black line", femoral neck, navicular, base 5th metatarsal, sesamoid\): Aggressive non\-WB; possible surgical fixation; risk of non\-union\.
- __Military data \(Wood et al\., Royal Marines\):__ Femoral/tibial 21\.1 weeks return; metatarsal 12\.2 weeks; __plus average 5 weeks after exercise restarts to reach pre\-injury level__\.
- __Return\-to\-running:__ Begin at 30–50% pre\-injury volume, 10% rule progression\.
- __Prevention:__ Adequate calcium \(1,000–1,500 mg\) \+ Vit D \(800–1,000 IU\); progressive volume; replace shoes every 300 mi; female athletes — screen for RED\-S/menstrual dysfunction\.

## <a id="_js6xbp508wc5"></a>__5\. TACTICAL / MILITARY\-SPECIFIC INJURIES__

### <a id="_38f6xl9zhmig"></a>__5\.1 Rucking Injuries__

__Biomechanics \(Knapik; Orthojournal review\):__

- Loads >25–30% body weight cause significant kinematic changes \(forward trunk lean ~10–15°, anterior pelvic tilt, increased lumbar load\)\.
- __Most common injuries:__ Low back, knee, foot/ankle \(blisters, metatarsalgia, stress fractures\), shoulders \(strap pressure, brachial plexus traction → "rucksack palsy"\)\.
- Hip belt __shifts load from shoulders to pelvis__ and reduces low back compression — use whenever tactically permissible\.
- Each kg added to feet costs 7–10% extra energy; each kg to thigh costs 4%\. Boots and load distribution matter\.

__Programming for rucking durability \(TSAC\):__

- Posterior chain strength: deadlift, hip thrust, RDL, KB swing, single\-leg RDL\.
- Heavy carries: farmer's carry, suitcase carry, front\-loaded carries \(zercher, sandbag\)\.
- Trunk anti\-flexion/extension/lateral: heavy planks, Pallof press, side plank, overhead carry\.
- __Progressive ruck:__ Start ~25 lb at 2\.5–3 mph; build by 5–10 lb every 2–3 weeks; cap weekly distance increase at ~10%\.

### <a id="_1b0757i18hsl"></a>__5\.2 Combat Carry / Load\-Bearing Equipment__

- Body armor \(~25 lb\) \+ ruck \(45–80\+ lb\) creates anterior weight that pulls into kyphosis; counter with rear\-deltoid, lower\-trap, and posterior chain emphasis\.
- Weapon carry: low\-ready position chronic loading creates anterior shoulder/biceps tendinopathy and pec minor tightness — daily T\-spine extension \+ scap retraction work \(band pull\-aparts 3×20\)\.

### <a id="_6f010ddmmj2e"></a>__5\.3 Jump Injuries \(Static\-Line Parachute Operations\)__

- __Injury rate:__ 0\.9–29 per 1,000 jumps depending on conditions; ~5\.5–13 per 1,000 typical \(Knapik\)\.
- __Mechanism:__ ~70% of injuries from improper Parachute Landing Fall \(PLF\)\. Ankle is most\-injured joint \(~33%\); 80% of injuries are lower extremity\.
- __Common patterns:__ Lateral malleolar fracture, posterior malleolar fracture \(Korean SOF data: 41% of ankle fractures\), lateral ankle sprain, knee MCL/meniscus, lumbar compression fracture \(heels\-first hard landings\)\.
- __Prevention:__ Ankle braces shown to reduce ankle injury incidence; PLF mastery; jump\-specific eccentric loading \(drop\-jump landings progressive\)\.

### <a id="_cwplwrvqj6jv"></a>__5\.4 PT Test Overuse \(Run\-Heavy Selection / Prep\)__

- __Shin splints__ \(4\.8\.3\), __plantar fasciitis__ \(4\.8\.2\), __patellar tendinopathy__ \(4\.6\.1\), __Achilles tendinopathy__ \(1\.4\) are dominant\.
- __Sudden activity ramp__ \(sedentary → BCT\) is the single largest predictor\.
- __Prevention strategies before reporting:__
	- 12\+ weeks of progressive running \(start where you are; don't go from 0 to 5 mi\)\.
	- Calf, tibialis, hip strengthening 2x/week\.
	- Replace shoes every 300–500 miles\.

### <a id="_qcql9orp81pu"></a>__5\.5 Operator Syndrome \(SOF Population\)__

Frueh et al\. 2020 \(*Int J Psychiatry Med*\); Rivera 2022; medRxiv 2025 validation:

A constellation in special operations forces from chronic high allostatic load:

- TBI / post\-concussive
- Endocrine dysfunction \(low T, thyroid\)
- Sleep disturbance, OSA
- Chronic joint/back pain & headaches
- Substance use, depression, suicidality
- Hypervigilance, marital/relational strain
- Existential/transition issues

For Gunny: Operator Syndrome is a *complex chronic* presentation, not a gym injury\. Gunny acknowledges it, validates the user, __strongly recommends specialized care__ \(VA Polytrauma, Intrepid Spirit Centers, MSOFC, SOF\-specific clinicians\), supports physical readiness foundations \(sleep, mobility, gentle progressive loading\), and avoids oversimplified solutions\.

### <a id="_t1mebc83f0is"></a>__5\.6 Army H2F \(Holistic Health and Fitness\)__

H2F is the Army's official model deploying performance teams \(PTs, ATCs, S&C coaches, dietitians, mental performance, chaplains\) at brigade level\. Five readiness pillars: physical, nutritional, mental, sleep, spiritual\. Designed because __MSK injuries account for ~65% of non\-deployable soldiers__\. Gunny aligns with H2F by treating users as tactical athletes across all five pillars\.

### <a id="_o8uy6toxn3j4"></a>__5\.7 Other Military Doctrine Sources__

- __MARSOC Human Performance Program__ and __SOCOM Tactical Athlete Program__ emphasize periodization and recovery integration\.
- __NSCA TSAC\-F / TSAC Report__ is the most comprehensive evidence\-based tactical S&C resource\.
- __Navy NSW \(SEAL\) PT__ — sometimes called "operational athletic training"; emphasis on water\-based recovery and durability\.

## <a id="_v0l3gbebzn7v"></a>__6\. RETURN\-TO\-TRAINING DECISION FRAMEWORK__

For any injury, Gunny applies a __criteria\-based, not time\-based__ decision \(Bern Consensus 2016\):

1. __Tissue healing milestone met__ \(timeline\-appropriate\)\.
2. __Pain ≤ 2/10__ during the activity being attempted\.
3. __Range of motion__ ≥ 90% of contralateral side or pre\-injury\.
4. __Strength symmetry__ Limb Symmetry Index ≥ 90% on hop tests / strength tests\.
5. __Confidence/psychological readiness__ — TSK <37, no significant kinesiophobia\.
6. __Sport\- or task\-specific test passed__ \(e\.g\., for ruck: 30 min @ 25% BW pain\-free; for run: walk\-jog\-run progression complete\)\.

Failing any one → not ready\. Time alone is insufficient evidence\.

## <a id="_fbig90ga54fr"></a>__9\. EVIDENCE\-LEVEL SUMMARY — KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR GUNNY__

__STRONG evidence \(Gunny can be confident\):__

- Progressive loading is the foundation of soft\-tissue rehab\.
- McGill Big 3 for non\-specific LBP\.
- Eccentric/HSR for tendinopathy \(Alfredson, Cook/Purdam, Beyer\)\.
- Hip\-strengthening for ITBS, PFP, SI dysfunction\.
- Sleep extension for performance and injury risk\.
- Criteria\-based RTS reduces ACL re\-injury\.
- Pain\-monitoring model \(Silbernagel\) permits training through tendinopathy\.

__MODERATE evidence \(use with judgment\):__

- Specific scapular stabilization for SAPS\.
- Dry needling for short\-term myofascial pain\.
- BFR for post\-op rehab\.
- McKenzie directional preference for disc\-related LBP\.
- High\-load strengthening for plantar fasciopathy \(single major RCT\)\.

__EMERGING / PRACTITIONER\-DRIVEN:__

- Joint\-by\-joint approach \(Boyle/Cook\)\.
- Kelly Starrett mobility framework\.
- ACWR as injury predictor \(challenged by Impellizzeri\)\.
- Heat therapy for muscle injury recovery\.

__OVERSOLD \(Gunny should be skeptical\):__

- Cold plunges as performance enhancers \(modest DOMS effect; can blunt hypertrophy\)\.
- Whole\-body cryotherapy \(no advantage over CWI\)\.
- Kinesio tape as structural support\.
- Foam rolling the IT band itself\.
- NSAIDs for tendinopathy or stress fractures\.
- Cortisone injections as long\-term solution for tendinopathy\.
- Sit\-ups and weighted spinal flexion for "core" in someone with disc issues\.
