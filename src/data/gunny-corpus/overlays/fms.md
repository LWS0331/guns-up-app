## <a id="_6x3o8veavvbc"></a>__\[CHUNK 01 — MASTER: HOW GUNNY READS THE SCREEN\]__

__Tags:__ intake, master\_protocol, verdict, voice\_anchor

The screen is intel, not a grade\. Seven movement patterns, three clearing tests\. Each pattern scored 0 to 3\. Composite tops out at 21\. We don't chase 21 — we chase competence, symmetry, and zero pain\.

How I read your card, in order: First, I scan for pain\. Any 0 anywhere — a movement test or a clearing test — and you're out of programming\. Full stop\. Medical referral\. We don't train through pain, we don't load around it, we don't stretch it out\. Pain is a signal, and the signal says stand down until a clinician clears you\. Second, I scan for asymmetry\. Right side scoring different than left on any unilateral test is a louder warning than a low bilateral score\. A 3 right / 1 left is more dangerous than a flat 2/2\. Asymmetry means your body has already picked a compensation pattern and is running on it\. That's how operators tear hamstrings, blow shoulders, and end up on profile\. Third, I look at the lowest score\. The weakest link sets the ceiling\. Doesn't matter if six tests are 3s — the one 1 dictates what you can load\. Fourth, I check the composite\. ≥17 is green\. 15–16 is yellow\. ≤14 is the injury\-risk threshold validated in NFL and Marine Corps cohorts \(Kiesel 2007, O'Connor 2011, Lisman 2013\)\. Below 14 means elevated injury risk — we restrict load until we move it\.

The verdict I deliver to you has four parts: __status__ \(green/yellow/red/medical\), __gates__ \(what stays locked\), __mission__ \(your corrective block\), and __re\-screen__ \(when we test again\)\.

I don't hedge\. I don't soften scores\. If you screened a 1 on the deep squat, the back squat is locked\. That's not punishment — that's how we keep you operational past 40\. We fix the pattern, we re\-screen, we unlock the load\. Move well, then move heavy\.

Standing rule from Gray Cook himself: don't add strength to dysfunction\. We follow that rule here without exception\.

## <a id="_ny5g2uynf0jf"></a>__\[CHUNK 02 — DEEP SQUAT: PROTOCOL, SCORING, COMPENSATIONS, CORRECTIVES\]__

__Tags:__ test\_01, deep\_squat, bilateral, mobility\_global

__Mission:__ Test bilateral hip, knee, and ankle mobility plus shoulder/T\-spine extension under symmetrical loading\.

__Kit:__ Dowel \(~48 in\)\. 2x6 board for the score\-of\-2 attempt\.

__Setup and call:__ Feet shoulder\-width, toes forward\. Dowel on top of the head, shoulders and elbows at 90° — that sets your grip\. Press the dowel directly overhead, elbows locked\. Maintain upright torso, heels and dowel position fixed\. Descend as deep as possible\. Hold one count\. Stand up\. Up to 3 attempts\. If you can't hit a 3 flat\-footed, we put your heels on the 2x6 and run it again\.

__Scoring:__

- __3__ — torso parallel to tibia or more vertical, femur below horizontal, knees over feet, dowel over feet, heels flat\.
- __2__ — same criteria met, but only with heels elevated on the 2x6\.
- __1__ — can't hit criteria even with heels elevated, or lumbar flexion appears\.
- __0__ — pain anywhere\. Refer out\.

__Compensations and what they tell me:__

- __Heels rising__ → ankle dorsiflexion restriction \(soleus, talocrural mortise\) or posterior chain length deficit\.
- __Forward torso lean / dowel over toes__ → thoracic extension restriction, lat/pec minor shortness, or shoulder flexion deficit\.
- __Knees caving \(valgus\)__ → glute med/max weakness or motor control fault\.
- __Butt wink \(lumbar flexion at depth\)__ → hamstring/posterior chain length, hip flexion ROM limit, or lumbopelvic control deficit\.
- __Toes flaring out__ → lateral gastroc tightness, weak medial chain\.

__Corrective hierarchy:__

- __Mobility \(daily\):__ knee\-to\-wall ankle drill 2x10/side; banded ankle distraction 2 min/side; 90/90 hip switch 2x8/side; frog rocking 2x10; foam roller T\-spine extension 8–10 reps × 3 segments; quadruped open book 2x8/side; assisted deep squat hold \(TRX/post\) 3x30–60 sec\.
- __Static stability \(3x/wk\):__ supine 90/90 breathing 5 breaths × 3; dead bug 2x8/side\.
- __Dynamic stability \(3x/wk\):__ prying goblet squat 2x5 × 5\-sec hold; tempo goblet squat \(3\-sec descent \+ 3\-sec hold\) 3x6\.
- __Pattern \(2x/wk\):__ heel\-elevated goblet squat 3x8 \(training wheels — wean off\); box squat to depth 3x6–8; toe\-touch\-to\-squat reverse pattern 2x6\.
- __Load \(when screen ≥2\):__ front squat 3x3–6, then back squat per main programming\.

Heel lifts are a crutch\. We use them to keep training, not to live in\. Re\-screen at 4 weeks\.

## <a id="_21eqrwm5zvga"></a>__\[CHUNK 03 — HURDLE STEP: PROTOCOL, SCORING, COMPENSATIONS, CORRECTIVES\]__

__Tags:__ test\_02, hurdle\_step, unilateral, single\_leg\_stability

__Mission:__ Test stride mechanics — stance\-leg single\-leg stability and step\-leg hip flexion/control\.

__Kit:__ Dowel and FMS hurdle\. Cord set to your tibial tuberosity height \(floor to top of the bony bump below the kneecap\)\.

__Setup and call:__ Feet together, toes touching the base of the test kit, directly under the cord\. Dowel across shoulders behind the neck — back\-rack carry\. Maintain upright posture\. Raise the test leg, dorsiflex the foot, step over the cord\. Touch heel to floor without weighting it\. Return to start in the same lane\. 3 reps per side\. Both sides scored\. Lower side recorded; asymmetry flagged\.

__Scoring:__

- __3__ — hips/knees/ankles aligned in the sagittal plane, no lumbar movement, dowel and cord stay parallel\.
- __2__ — alignment lost, lumbar movement, or dowel/cord not parallel\.
- __1__ — foot contacts cord, or loss of balance\.
- __0__ — pain\. Refer out\.

__Compensations and what they tell me:__

- __Toe catches the cord__ → step\-leg hip or ankle ROM limit, or balance failure forcing low clearance\.
- __Stance heel rises__ → stance\-leg ankle dorsiflexion restriction\.
- __Lumbar flexion / forward lean \(dowel tips forward\)__ → step\-leg hip flexion limit or trunk stabilizer weakness\.
- __Lumbar extension / arch__ → stance\-leg hip flexor tightness pulling pelvis into anterior tilt\.
- __Hip drop on step side \(Trendelenburg\)__ → stance\-leg glute med weakness\.
- __Trunk sway lateral__ → frontal\-plane core/hip stability failure\.
- __Asymmetry \(3R/1L\)__ → relative bilateral hip mobility mismatch — clinically loud, since one hip works max flexion while the other works max extension\.

__Corrective hierarchy:__

- __Mobility \(daily\):__ half\-kneeling hip flexor stretch 2x30 sec/side; couch stretch 2 min/side on restricted leg; knee\-to\-wall ankle DF 2x10/side\.
- __Static stability \(3x/wk\):__ single\-leg stance progression — eyes open → eyes closed → on foam \(3x30 sec/leg\); half\-kneeling chops and lifts 2x8/side; supine reverse\-pattern hurdle step 2x8/side; leg lock bridge 2x8/side\.
- __Dynamic stability \(2–3x/wk\):__ single\-leg deadlift \(light KB\) 2x6/side; half\-kneeling pelvic dissociation 2x10\.
- __Pattern \(2x/wk\):__ marching with 3\-sec hold 2x10/leg; low\-box step\-up \(heel\-driven, no back\-leg push\-off\) 3x6/side; resisted hip flexion 2x10/side\.
- __Load \(when screen ≥2 bilateral\):__ loaded step\-up 3x6–8/side; rear\-foot\-elevated split squat; pistol progression\.

## <a id="_pg26qyzvyy"></a>__\[CHUNK 04 — IN\-LINE LUNGE: PROTOCOL, SCORING, COMPENSATIONS, CORRECTIVES\]__

__Tags:__ test\_03, inline\_lunge, unilateral, split\_stance, anti\_rotation

__Mission:__ Stress split\-stance hip mobility, ankle DF, T\-spine rotation, and frontal\-plane trunk control under a narrow base\.

__Kit:__ 2x6 board\. Dowel\. Tibial length measurement \(re\-use from hurdle step\)\.

__Setup and call:__ Step onto the board, front foot flat, toe at the 0 mark\. Back heel at the tibial\-length mark\. Both toes forward, both feet flat\. Dowel vertical along the spine, three contact points: back of head, upper back, sacrum\. Hand opposite the front foot grips high \(cervical\); same\-side hand grips low \(lumbar\)\. Maintain upright posture and dowel contact\. Drop into a lunge — back knee touches the board behind your front heel\. Return\. 3 reps per side\. Front leg identifies the side scored\. Lower side recorded; asymmetry flagged\.

__Scoring:__

- __3__ — three dowel contacts maintained, dowel vertical, no torso movement, dowel/feet stay sagittal, knee touches behind front heel\.
- __2__ — any of those criteria fails\.
- __1__ — loss of balance\.
- __0__ — pain\. Refer out\.

__Compensations and what they tell me:__

- __Dowel tilts forward / sacrum loses contact__ → T\-spine extension or back\-leg hip extension restriction \(rectus femoris/iliopsoas tightness\)\.
- __Dowel tilts backward__ → kyphotic posture, weak posterior chain hold\.
- __Trunk rotation \(dowel/feet leave sagittal\)__ → trunk anti\-rotation deficit, T\-spine rotation asymmetry\.
- __Lateral lean__ → frontal\-plane stance\-hip stability failure\.
- __Front knee valgus__ → front\-leg hip abductor weakness\.
- __Back knee won't reach board__ → back\-leg hip extension/quad length restriction or front\-leg ankle/knee mobility deficit\.
- __Front heel rises__ → front\-leg ankle DF restriction\.
- __Loss of balance__ → narrow\-base stability failure\.

__Corrective hierarchy:__

- __Mobility \(daily\):__ couch stretch 2x60 sec/side; half\-kneeling ankle rocker 2x10/side; quadruped open book / Brettzel 2x8/side; adductor rock\-back 2x10/side\.
- __Static stability \(3x/wk\):__ half\-kneeling Pallof press 3x8/side × 5\-sec hold; half\-kneeling KB overhead hold 3x20 sec/side; tall\-kneeling Pallof press 3x8/side; half\-kneeling chop and lift 2x8/side\.
- __Dynamic stability \(2x/wk\):__ half\-to\-tall\-kneeling transitions, weighted, 2x6/side; reverse lunge from deficit, 3\-sec eccentric, 2x6/side\.
- __Pattern \(2x/wk\):__ TRX\-supported split squat 3x8/side; reverse lunge unloaded 3x8/side; walking lunge unloaded 2x8/leg\.
- __Load \(when screen ≥2 bilateral\):__ goblet reverse lunge → DB walking lunge → Bulgarian split squat → barbell split squat\.

## <a id="_gbtgs0kr15q7"></a>__\[CHUNK 05 — SHOULDER MOBILITY: PROTOCOL, SCORING, COMPENSATIONS, CORRECTIVES\]__

__Tags:__ test\_04, shoulder\_mobility, unilateral, has\_clearing\_test, upper\_body\_mobility

__Mission:__ Test combined glenohumeral mobility — one shoulder in flexion/abduction/external rotation while the other works extension/adduction/internal rotation\. Adds T\-spine extension and scapular control\.

__Kit:__ Tape measure for hand length \(distal wrist crease to tip of middle finger\)\.

__Setup and call:__ Stand tall, feet together\. Make fists with thumbs tucked inside\. In one motion — one fist goes overhead and down the back, the other goes behind the back and up the spine\. No creeping\. Tester measures the distance between the two closest knuckles, in units of your hand length\. Both sides run\. The flexed \(overhead\) shoulder identifies the side scored\. Lower side recorded; asymmetry flagged\.

__Scoring:__

- __3__ — fists within one hand length\.
- __2__ — fists within one and a half hand lengths\.
- __1__ — fists farther than one and a half hand lengths\.
- __0__ — pain during the reach OR pain on the shoulder impingement clearing test \(see Chunk 09\)\.

__Tip:__ if distance equals hand length exactly, score low\.

__Compensations and what they tell me:__

- __Hard asymmetry \(e\.g\., 3R/1L\)__ → unilateral capsule/cuff restriction, classic in dominant\-arm overhead athletes \(gained ER, lost IR\)\.
- __Lumbar extension/rib flare to reach__ → T\-spine extension limit; operator borrows from the low back\.
- __Forward\-rounded shoulders, can't bring fists together overhead__ → pec minor / lat shortness, anterior capsule tightness\.
- __Scapular winging or poor upward rotation__ → scapulothoracic dysfunction; if the scap doesn't move, the GH joint pays\.

__Corrective hierarchy:__

- __Mobility \(daily\):__ foam roller T\-spine extension 8–10 reps × 3 segments; quadruped open book / Brettzel 2\.0 3 breaths × 5/side; lat foam roll 60–90 sec/side; puppy pose / passive lat hang 3x30 sec; door\-frame pec stretch at 3 heights 30 sec each; pec minor lacrosse ball 2 min/side; band/PVC dislocates 2x10–15; sleeper stretch 3x30 sec on the IR\-restricted side; cross\-body stretch 3x30 sec/side\.
- __Static stability \(3x/wk\):__ prone Y/T/W on bench 2x8/letter × 5–10 sec hold; wall slide with foam roller 2x10; band no\-money drill 2x12–15; back\-to\-wall shoulder flexion 2x10; side\-lying ER \(light DB\) 2x12\.
- __Dynamic stability \(2–3x/wk\):__ bear crawl 3x10–20 yards; KB armbar 3x30–45 sec/side; push\-up plus 3x10\.
- __Pattern \(2x/wk\):__ half\-kneeling bottoms\-up KB press 3x5/side; Turkish get\-up partial\-to\-full 3x1–3/side; landmine press 3x6–8/side\.
- __Load \(when SM ≥2 bilateral and clearing test negative\):__ strict OHP 3x3–6; push press / jerk 3x2–5\.

## <a id="_yfynldg1lued"></a>__\[CHUNK 06 — ACTIVE STRAIGHT\-LEG RAISE: PROTOCOL, SCORING, COMPENSATIONS, CORRECTIVES\]__

__Tags:__ test\_05, aslr, unilateral, mobility\_priority, hip\_dissociation

__Mission:__ Dissociate the moving leg from the trunk while keeping the down leg quiet\. Tests active hamstring/posterior chain length AND down\-leg hip extension capability AND core control\. Cook flags ASLR as the highest\-priority screen — fix it first\.

__Kit:__ Dowel, 2x6 board\.

__Setup and call:__ Lie supine, palms up, head on the floor\. Back of the knees on the 2x6, toes pointing straight up\. Tester places the dowel vertical at the midpoint between your down\-leg ASIS and the middle of your kneecap — that's the reference plumb line\. Pull toes of the test foot toward your shin\. Knee straight\. Raise the test leg as high as possible\. Down leg keeps back of knee on the 2x6, toes up, knee locked, head down\. 3 reps per side\. Moving limb identifies the side scored\. Lower side recorded; asymmetry flagged\.

__Scoring:__

- __3__ — vertical line of test malleolus falls between mid\-thigh and ASIS of the down leg; down leg neutral\.
- __2__ — malleolus falls between mid\-thigh and the knee joint line; down leg neutral\.
- __1__ — malleolus below the knee joint line\.
- __0__ — pain\. Refer out\.

__Tip:__ dowel exactly at mid\-thigh = score low\.

__Compensations and what they tell me:__

- __Down\-leg knee bends / lifts off the 2x6__ → opposite\-side hip flexor tightness \(iliopsoas/rec fem\) pulling pelvis into anterior tilt\. Counterintuitive but common\.
- __Down\-leg toes externally rotate__ → loss of pelvic neutral; invalidate the trial\.
- __Head lifts / arms scrub the floor__ → poor anterior core stability\.
- __Lumbar flattens \(posterior pelvic tilt\)__ → hamstring length deficit on test leg producing pelvic motion, not real hip flexion\.
- __Test knee bends__ → hamstring shortness; knee flex shortens the chain to fake hip flexion height\.
- __Asymmetry__ → classic Janda lower\-cross — one\-side hamstring short, opposite\-side hip flexor short\.

__Corrective hierarchy:__

- __Mobility \(daily\):__ supine band hamstring stretch with PNF contract\-relax 3x30 sec \+ 3x6\-sec contracts; 90/90 hamstring 3x10 slow; sciatic nerve flossing 2x10 cycles/side; couch stretch on the down\-leg side 2x60 sec/side; toe\-touch progression with towel\-under\-heels then towel\-under\-toes 5 reps × 2 rounds\.
- __Static stability \(3–4x/wk\):__ supine 90/90 breathing 5 × 3; dead bug full progression \(basic → banded → loaded\) 2x8/side; supine heel slides 2x10/side; leg lowers 2x10\.
- __Dynamic stability \(3x/wk\):__ banded reverse\-ASLR \(Takasaki 2021 — outperforms repeated ASLR\) 2x8/side; happy\-baby progression 2x6 cycles; leg lock bridge 2x8/side\.
- __Pattern \(3x/wk during corrective phase\):__ dowel\-on\-spine hip hinge → wall hinge → KB deadlift to box 3x8\.
- __Load \(when ASLR ≥2 bilateral\):__ RDL, KB deadlift, conventional deadlift, KB swing\. ASLR gates all loaded hip\-hinge\.

ASLR clears before Shoulder Mobility\. Lower body resolves first because gait, hinge, squat, and even shoulder mechanics ride the kinetic chain upward\.

## <a id="_nj1nah9x4pby"></a>__\[CHUNK 07 — TRUNK STABILITY PUSH\-UP: PROTOCOL, SCORING, COMPENSATIONS, CORRECTIVES\]__

__Tags:__ test\_06, tspu, bilateral, has\_clearing\_test, anti\_extension

__Mission:__ Test reflexive sagittal\-plane spine stability under a symmetrical push\. This is a stability test, not a strength test\. Body lifts as one rigid unit or it fails\.

__Kit:__ Floor only\.

__Setup and call:__ Lie prone\. Arms extended overhead, hands shoulder\-width\. Toes dorsiflexed\. Knees locked\. Head neutral\.

- __Men:__ thumbs aligned with the top of the forehead\.
- __Women:__ thumbs aligned with the chin\.

From dead stop, push the body up as one rigid unit into a top\-of\-push\-up position\. Chest and stomach come off the floor at the same time\. No lumbar lag\. 2 attempts at the score\-3 hand position\. If failed, drop hands to the score\-2 position \(men: chin; women: clavicle\), 2 more attempts\.

__Scoring:__

- __3__ — body rises as one unit, no spinal lag, men's thumbs at forehead / women's at chin\.
- __2__ — body rises as one unit, no lag, men's thumbs at chin / women's at clavicle\.
- __1__ — can't lift cleanly even at the score\-2 hand position\.
- __0__ — pain during the push OR pain on the spinal extension clearing test \(see Chunk 10\)\.

__Compensations and what they tell me:__

- __Hips lag / chest lifts first__ — the signature failure\. Anterior core/lumbar stabilizer reflex weakness; transverse abdominis and obliques aren't co\-contracting\.
- __Hips lift first / "worm"__ → core firing dysfunction; can't brake spinal extension\.
- __Excessive lumbar extension during push / rib flare__ → anterior core insufficiency; lats and erectors dominating\.
- __Hands creep down before push__ → can't generate stability at the prescribed lever; tester must enforce hand position\.
- __Head jut forward__ → cervical compensation for poor anterior chain\.

A failed TSPU means kinetic energy will leak through your lumbar spine in real\-world tasks — overhead press, jerk, ruck stride, sprint\.

__Corrective hierarchy:__

- __Mobility \(daily\):__ foam roller T\-spine extension 2x10; back\-to\-wall shoulder flexion 2x10; couch stretch 2x60 sec/side\.
- __Static stability \(3–4x/wk\):__ dead bug full progression 3x8/side; hollow body hold 3x20–45 sec; hard\-style plank 5x10 sec maximal tension; quadruped/tall\-kneeling diaphragmatic breathing 5 breaths × 3\.
- __Dynamic stability \(3x/wk\):__ long\-lever / RKC plank 3x20–30 sec; ab wheel rollout from knees → standing 3x6–10; bird\-dog with 5\-sec hold 2x8/side\.
- __Pattern \(2–3x/wk\):__ incline push\-up with bar progressively lowered 3x8–10; dead\-stop bottoms\-up push\-up 3x5; push\-up to plank \(hands → forearms\) 3x6; walk\-out \+ push\-up 3x5\.
- __Load \(when TSPU ≥2 and clearing test negative\):__ weighted push\-up, bench press, OHP, dip per main programming\.

## <a id="_wv1c7upmkzp2"></a>__\[CHUNK 08 — ROTARY STABILITY: PROTOCOL, SCORING, COMPENSATIONS, CORRECTIVES\]__

__Tags:__ test\_07, rotary\_stability, unilateral, has\_clearing\_test, multiplane\_core

__Mission:__ Test asymmetric trunk stability across sagittal AND transverse planes during simultaneous upper\- and lower\-extremity motion\. Most complex test in the screen\.

__Kit:__ 2x6 board\.

__Setup and call:__ Quadruped over the 2x6 — board running between your hands and knees in your long axis\. Hands under shoulders, knees under hips, all at 90°\. Spine flat\. Toes tucked \(dorsiflexed\)\. Thumbs, knees, and toes contact the sides of the board\.

__Score\-3 \(unilateral / ipsilateral\) pattern:__ same\-side arm and leg fly out — shoulder flexes forward, hip and knee extend backward, like flying\. Without touching down, flex shoulder and knee to bring elbow and knee together over the board\. Return to extended fly\. Return to start\. Limbs stay over the board — no major weight shift\.

__Score\-2 \(diagonal / contralateral\) pattern:__ opposite arm and opposite leg\. Same fly → elbow\-to\-knee touch over the board\.

3 reps unilateral, then 3 reps diagonal if needed\. Both sides scored\. Upper extremity identifies the side scored\. Lower side recorded; asymmetry flagged\.

__Scoring:__

- __3__ — clean unilateral rep, no major weight shift off the board\.
- __2__ — clean diagonal rep\.
- __1__ — can't perform diagonal cleanly\.
- __0__ — pain during movement OR pain on the spinal flexion clearing test \(see Chunk 11\)\.

__Compensations and what they tell me:__

- __Major weight shift off the 2x6__ → frontal\-plane core stability failure under rotational demand\.
- __Pelvis rotates / hips tilt__ → transverse\-plane stability deficit \(obliques, QL\)\.
- __Spine rounds or hyperextends during the elbow\-knee touch__ → poor segmental control under rotation\.
- __Loss of balance / collapse__ → motor control failure at the proximal core\.
- __Asymmetry__ — major flag\. Rotational control deficits predispose lumbar and lower\-extremity injury\.

__Corrective hierarchy:__

- __Mobility \(daily\):__ quadruped open book / side\-lying open book / Brettzel 2x8/side; 90/90 hip switch 2x8/side; couch stretch 2x60 sec/side\.
- __Static stability \(3x/wk\):__ quadruped diagonal reach \(hand only, then leg only, then combined\) 2x8/side; bird\-dog progression — basic → banded → KB\-loaded — 2x8/side × 3\-sec hold; dead bug variants 2x8/side; rolling patterns \(upper\-lead, lower\-lead, both directions\) 2x3 each\.
- __Dynamic stability \(2–3x/wk\):__ bear crawl forward/backward/lateral 3x10–20 yards; crab walk 2x10 yards each; side plank with leg lift 2x8/side; stability\-ball stir\-the\-pot 3x8 each direction; RNT bird\-dog 2x8/side\.
- __Pattern \(3x/wk\):__ Pallof press progression — tall\-kneeling → half\-kneeling → split → standing → single\-leg — 2x8/side × 5\-sec hold; cable chops and lifts in all postures 2x8/side; Turkish get\-up 3x1–3/side\.
- __Load \(when RS ≥2 bilateral and clearing test negative\):__ suitcase carry 3x30–40 yards/side; racked carry 3x30–40 yards/side; overhead carry 3x20–30 yards/side; rotational med ball throws 4x4/side; single\-arm DL, single\-arm row\.

## <a id="_xzbo01w7vxrw"></a>__\[CHUNK 09 — SHOULDER IMPINGEMENT CLEARING TEST\]__

__Tags:__ clearing\_test, shoulder, pain\_flag, gates\_shoulder\_mobility

__When:__ Immediately after Shoulder Mobility, both sides\.

__Setup:__ Stand tall\. Place your palm flat on the opposite shoulder\. Keep palm in contact\. Lift the elbow straight up toward the ceiling, as high as it goes\. Repeat opposite side\.

__Call to operator:__ "Palm to the opposite shoulder\. Drive the elbow toward the ceiling\. Tell me if you feel pain — front, top, or back of the shoulder\."

__Reading the result:__

- __Negative \(no pain\)__ → Shoulder Mobility raw score stands\.
- __Positive \(pain anywhere in the shoulder\)__ → Shoulder Mobility scored 0 on that side\. Refer out\.

__What this catches:__ Subacromial impingement\. The combined flexion \+ horizontal adduction \+ internal rotation reproduces the Hawkins\-Kennedy provocation\. The mobility reach alone can score a 3 and still hide an impingement — that's why the clearing test exists\.

__Standing rule:__ Pain on this clearing test is non\-negotiable\. We don't program around it, mobilize it, or stretch through it\. Operator goes to a clinician — orthopedic or sports medicine — for evaluation\. Once cleared, we re\-screen and program from there\.

Until cleared: no overhead pressing, no push press, no jerk, no snatch, no pull\-ups under load\. Lower\-body work continues if otherwise indicated\. Anti\-extension core, hip mobility, and lower\-body stability work proceed normally\.

## <a id="_kt5k8kvk2s9m"></a>__\[CHUNK 10 — SPINAL EXTENSION CLEARING TEST \(PRESS\-UP\)\]__

__Tags:__ clearing\_test, spine, pain\_flag, gates\_tspu

__When:__ Immediately after the Trunk Stability Push\-Up\.

__Setup:__ Stay prone\. Hands flat on the floor directly under the shoulders, palms down\. Hips and pelvis stay on the floor\. Press the elbows fully straight, lift the chest as high as it goes\. Lumbar spine falls into extension\. Pelvis stays grounded — passive press\-up, not an active back extension\.

__Call to operator:__ "Hands under shoulders\. Press the chest up by straightening your arms\. Hips stay down\. Tell me if you feel low back pain\."

__Reading the result:__

- __Negative \(no pain\)__ → TSPU raw score stands\.
- __Positive \(low back pain\)__ → TSPU scored 0\. Refer out\.

__What this catches:__ Lumbar pathology that loads in extension — facet irritation, spondylolisthesis, posterior element pain\. May also clear sacroiliac and thoracolumbar junction\. Extension intolerance under load is a hard predictor of injury during overhead pressing and any spine\-loaded lift\.

__Until cleared:__ No loaded overhead pressing, no push press, no jerk, no heavy back squats \(extension demand under bar\), no kettlebell swings \(terminal extension at top\)\. Anti\-extension core \(dead bug, hollow hold, plank\) proceeds\. Flexion\-bias hip mobility proceeds\. Lower\-body unilateral work without spinal loading proceeds\.

## <a id="_beh6udweib5o"></a>__\[CHUNK 11 — SPINAL FLEXION CLEARING TEST \(POSTERIOR ROCK\)\]__

__Tags:__ clearing\_test, spine, pain\_flag, gates\_rotary\_stability

__When:__ Immediately after Rotary Stability\.

__Setup:__ Quadruped — hands under shoulders, knees under hips\. Rock the hips back to the heels\. End position: buttocks touching heels, chest touching thighs\. Hands stay forward, reaching out as far as possible\. Child's pose, loaded\.

__Call to operator:__ "Sit your hips back to your heels\. Chest to thighs\. Reach your hands out long\. Tell me if you feel low back pain\."

__Reading the result:__

- __Negative \(no pain\)__ → Rotary Stability raw score stands\.
- __Positive \(low back pain\)__ → Rotary Stability scored 0\. Refer out\.

__What this catches:__ Flexion\-bias lumbar pathology — disc\-related symptoms, posterior element strain, neural tension under flexion load\. End\-range flexion intolerance is a hard predictor of injury under bent\-over loaded lifts and rotational loading\.

__Until cleared:__ No loaded carries with rotational demand, no heavy bent\-over rows, no good mornings, no rotational med ball throws, no heavy deadlifts \(the start position loads spinal flexion\)\. Anti\-rotation work in neutral spine \(Pallof press, suitcase carries at light load with strict neutral\) proceeds with caution\. Bird\-dog and dead bug proceed\. Hip and T\-spine mobility proceed\.

## <a id="_450eh6mj2ssj"></a>__\[CHUNK 12 — CORRECTIVE HIERARCHY: GRAY COOK STANDARD\]__

__Tags:__ philosophy, hierarchy, programming\_logic, voice\_anchor

The hierarchy is not optional\. It runs in this order, every time:

__1\. Mobility \(passive ROM\)\.__ Restore the joint range first\. You can't stabilize a range you don't own\. Tools: soft tissue \(foam roll, lacrosse ball, IASTM\), PNF stretching, joint mobilization, breathing drills, dynamic mobility\. Daily, often multiple times per day\.

__2\. Static stability / motor control \(non\-weight\-bearing\)\.__ Once the range exists, teach the brain to control it from a safe position — supine, prone, quadruped, half\-/tall\-kneeling\. Dead bug, bird\-dog with hold, supine pelvic control, breathing drills\. 3–4x/week\.

__3\. Dynamic stability / motor control \(weight\-bearing\)\.__ Challenge the new range under bodyweight load with movement\. Rolling, chops and lifts, RNT variations, single\-leg balance with reach\. 3–4x/week\.

__4\. Pattern\.__ Re\-pattern the actual movement — squat, hinge, lunge, push, carry — under cognitive load with regression or assistance\. Place the operator in a posture where it can only be done correctly\. Goblet squat, Turkish get\-up, single\-arm farmer's carry\. 2–3x/week\.

__5\. Load\.__ Add external resistance\. Strength, power, sport\-specific demands\. The deadlift, the back squat, the overhead press\. 1–2x/week per major lift\.

__The rules — burn them in:__

- __Don't add strength to dysfunction\.__ Aggressive loading on a broken pattern reinforces the compensation\. You make it worse, not better\.
- __Mobility before stability\.__ You cannot stabilize a range you don't have\.
- __ASLR before Shoulder Mobility\.__ Lower body resolves first because gait, hinge, and squat propagate upward\. Cook calls ASLR the highest priority of the screen\.
- __Address the lowest score first\.__ The composite is informational\. The diagnostic value is in the individual pattern\. Pull the weakest link up\.
- __Address asymmetry before symmetric low scores\.__ A 3R/1L is louder than a 2/2\. Asymmetry forces compensation, and compensation creates downstream injuries\.
- __Pain trumps everything\.__ Score 0 = out of programming\. Refer\.
- __Sometimes what we remove matters more than what we add\.__ If the operator can't squat well, take loaded squats out before piling on correctives\.

__Cook's algorithm for which pattern to fix first across the screen:__ ASLR → Shoulder Mobility → Rotary Stability → Trunk Stability Push\-Up → In\-Line Lunge → Hurdle Step → Deep Squat\. Mobility first\. Stability second\. Functional patterns last — they often self\-correct as upstream deficits resolve\.

__Cook's wisdom we operate by:__ "First move well, then move often\." That's the whole game\.

__Reading rule:__ Pain or dysfunction at a stability joint is usually a mobility loss at the adjacent mobile joint above or below\.

- __Knee pain__ is often an ankle problem \(stiff DF\) or a hip problem \(weak abductors, tight rotators\)\.
- __Lumbar pain__ is often a hip problem \(lost extension, lost rotation\) or T\-spine problem \(lost extension, lost rotation\)\.
- __Shoulder pain__ is often a scapular stability problem or T\-spine mobility problem\.

This is why we don't chase symptoms\. A failed deep squat with knees caving doesn't mean we drill knee stability — it means we look at the ankles and hips first\. A failed shoulder mobility test isn't always a shoulder fix — sometimes it's a T\-spine fix\. Diagnose up and down the chain\.

## <a id="_lc51mej52nwp"></a>__\[CHUNK 14 — COMPOSITE SCORING AND HARD GATES\]__

__Tags:__ composite, gates, programming\_lock, injury\_thresholds

__Composite tiers:__

- __≥17 — Green light\.__ Full programming authorized\. Active\-duty mean per Teyhen 2014 is 16\.2 ± 2\.2 — operators ≥17 are at or above one SD above population mean\.
- __15–16 — Yellow\.__ Program with monitoring\. Cap loaded RM% at ~80% on barbell movements until specific deficits are addressed\. Re\-screen at 4–6 weeks\.
- __≤14 — Red\. Injury risk threshold\.__ Hard restrictions\. Active corrective phase mandatory\. Re\-screen at 6–8 weeks\.
- __Any score = 0 → full medical referral, all gates locked on the affected pattern\.__
- __Any score = 1 → that pattern's loaded variants are locked\.__
- __Any unilateral asymmetry ≥1 point → unilateral loaded variants locked on that test's pattern; bilateral loaded variants permitted with monitoring\.__

__The evidence underneath:__

- Kiesel 2007 \(NFL\): composite ≤14 → odds ratio 11\.67 for serious injury\.
- Kiesel 2014 \(NFL larger cohort\): ≤14 → RR 1\.87\. __Asymmetry alone → RR 1\.80, independent of composite\.__ The combination of ≤14 \+ asymmetry → specificity 0\.87 for predicting injury\.
- O'Connor 2011 \(Marine officer candidates, n=874\): ≤14 → 1\.91× injury risk in short\-cycle, 1\.65× in long\-cycle\.
- Lisman 2013 \(same cohort\): ≤14 \+ 3\-mile run >20\.5 min → 4\.2× injury risk\. Stack movement and aerobic gates for hardest signal\.
- Bonazza 2017 meta: pooled OR 2\.74 at ≤14\.
- Moran 2017 meta: military male sub\-cohort RR 1\.47 \(the strongest signal of any sub\-population analyzed\)\.

__FMS sensitivity is low \(~0\.24\); specificity is moderate\-to\-high \(~0\.85–0\.91\)\.__ Operational read: a low score is a strong rule\-IN for elevated risk\. A high score is NOT a rule\-OUT — high screeners still get hurt\. We use the composite to deny load, not to bless it\.

__Hard gates \(movement → required clearance\):__

For each locked movement, the operator runs the corrective tier from the parent test's chunk\. Substitutes during lockout: heel\-elevated goblet squat for back squat; KB deadlift to box for conventional pull; landmine press for OHP; goblet split squat for loaded lunge; suitcase carry at light load for heavier rotational work; sled drag for sprint\.

## <a id="_urahs43tbncu"></a>__\[CHUNK 15 — PAIN FLAG PROTOCOL AND MEDICAL REFERRAL\]__

__Tags:__ pain, score\_zero, medical\_referral, hard\_stop

A score of 0 means pain\. Pain means stand down\.

__What triggers a 0:__

- Pain anywhere during a movement test \(deep squat, hurdle step, in\-line lunge, shoulder mobility reach, ASLR, TSPU, rotary stability\)\.
- Pain anywhere during a clearing test \(shoulder impingement, spinal extension press\-up, spinal flexion posterior rock\)\.

__What happens when a 0 fires:__

1. The painful pattern's score is 0, regardless of the underlying movement quality\. Even if the operator could otherwise hit a 3 on raw movement, pain overrides\.
2. Programming for that pattern is locked\. No correctives, no load, no "training around it\."
3. Operator is referred to a qualified clinician — orthopedic physician, sports medicine, physical therapist trained in SFMA \(Selective Functional Movement Assessment\) or equivalent\.
4. Other patterns continue if otherwise indicated\. A positive shoulder clearing test does not stop ASLR work\. A positive spinal flexion clearing test does not stop shoulder work\. Localize the lockout\.
5. Re\-screen only after written or verbal clinician clearance\. Document the clearance\.

__Why this is non\-negotiable:__ Cook is direct on this — the FMS is not designed for individuals with pain\. Pain changes motor control, biases compensation, and indicates underlying pathology that corrective exercise alone cannot resolve\. Pushing through a flagged pain signal is how operators turn a 4\-week clinical hold into a 6\-month surgical recovery\.

__Operator\-facing call:__ "You flagged pain on \[test\]\. That's a hard stop on \[movement category\]\. You're not in trouble — you're on the right path\. Get eyes on it from a clinician\. Until I see clearance, we keep the rest of your programming moving and we leave \[movement category\] alone\. Asking you to push through pain is how I lose operators\. Not how I keep them in the fight\."

__What "pain" means and what it doesn't:__

- Pain = sharp, stabbing, pinching, deep aching that the operator identifies as unusual or painful\.
- Stretching sensation, muscular fatigue, mild discomfort under exertion = NOT pain\. Score the movement, don't flag\.
- Operator hesitancy or fear of movement without identified pain = score the movement, document the hesitancy as motor control concern, not as pain\.

When in doubt, ask: "Sharp or stretch?" Sharp is a 0\. Stretch is not\.

## <a id="_kjusdnk9f57v"></a>__\[CHUNK 16 — REASSESSMENT CADENCE\]__

__Tags:__ rescreen, timeline, intervention\_response, programming\_review

We re\-screen on a fixed cadence\. Adaptation takes time, and hammering re\-tests every week chases noise instead of signal\.

__Standard cadence:__

- __4 weeks__ — interim spot\-check during active corrective phase\. Component\-level only — re\-test the failed patterns, not the full screen\. This is a tracking checkpoint, not a gate change\.
- __6 weeks__ — minimum interval for meaningful FMS change in trained tactical operators\. Goss et al\. 2009 \(SOF, n=90\): focused functional training produced \+2\.5 FMS points in 6 weeks\. Use this as the floor\.
- __8 weeks__ — standard full re\-screen\. Modal duration in successful intervention studies \(Clark 2022 systematic review: mean improvement \+3\.28 points across 6–8 week programs\)\. This is the default\.
- __12 weeks__ — when initial deficits are severe \(multiple 1s, multiple asymmetries\)\. More tissue/motor\-control change required\.
- __Annual__ — full re\-screen as part of operator readiness baseline\.

__What does NOT count:__

- Re\-tests at <4 weeks\. The change is noise\. Don't move gates on it\.
- Re\-tests after a deload\-only week\. Mobility may transiently change without true motor control adaptation\. Confirm at 4\+ weeks\.

__Interpretation rules:__

- If a 1 has not moved to a 2 in 4 weeks of focused corrective work, the corrective selection is wrong, not the operator\. Re\-evaluate the prescription\.
- If a screen improves by 1 point but the operator hasn't graduated through the corrective tier \(mobility → static → dynamic → pattern\), do not unlock load yet\. Score change without tier completion is fragile\.
- Frost et al\. 2012 \(firefighters, 12 weeks\): non\-targeted general strength training produced no group\-level FMS change\. Generic conditioning does not move the screen\. Targeted correctives do\.

__App\-level enforcement:__ auto\-prompt the operator for a re\-screen at 4\-week interim and 8\-week full intervals during corrective phases\. For green\-light operators \(≥17 with no asymmetry\), prompt re\-screen at 12 weeks and annually\.

## <a id="_fs2gnzbd0y58"></a>__\[CHUNK 17 — ASYMMETRY PROTOCOL\]__

__Tags:__ asymmetry, unilateral, priority\_rule, injury\_risk

__The rule:__ Asymmetry between sides on any unilateral test is treated as an independent injury risk factor and addressed before symmetric low scores\.

__The unilateral tests:__ Hurdle Step, In\-Line Lunge, Shoulder Mobility, ASLR, Rotary Stability\.

__The evidence:__

- Kiesel et al\. 2014 \(NFL\): ≥1 asymmetry → RR 1\.80 for time\-loss injury, independent of composite\. A high composite with an asymmetry still elevates risk\.
- Chalmers 2017 \(junior athletes\): ≥2 asymmetries → HR 3\.7 \(95% CI 1\.6–8\.6\)\. In that cohort, asymmetry was a stronger predictor than composite ≤14\.
- Cook's clinical observation: a 3R/1L is more dangerous than a 2/2\. The dysfunctional side is operating with a compensation pattern that the sound side masks\.

__How asymmetry reads on the score sheet:__

- Score the lower side as the "Final Score" that contributes to composite\.
- Document both raw scores\. Always\.
- Flag the asymmetry as a separate finding even when both sides are ≥2\.

__Programming response to asymmetry:__

1. __Lock the loaded unilateral variant__ of the test's pattern\.  

	- HS asymmetry → loaded single\-leg work \(pistol, RFE split squat, single\-leg DL\) locked\.
	- ILL asymmetry → loaded split squat, walking lunge with load locked\.
	- SM asymmetry → loaded unilateral pressing locked \(single\-arm OHP, single\-arm landmine, KB armbar with load\)\.
	- ASLR asymmetry → single\-leg deadlift loaded locked; symmetric hinge can proceed if both sides ≥2\.
	- RS asymmetry → loaded asymmetric carries \(single\-arm/single\-side suitcase, racked, OH\) locked on the deficit side\.
2. __Feed work to the deficit side\.__ Do NOT train both sides equally\. The standard is 2:1 corrective volume to the worse side\. Some practitioners run deficit side ONLY for 4 weeks, re\-screen, then re\-introduce balanced work\.  

3. __Permit bilateral loaded variants with monitoring__ when both sides ≥2\. A symmetric deficit \(ankle DF bilaterally restricted\) is different from an asymmetric one and is programmed differently\.  

4. __Re\-screen the asymmetry every 4 weeks until resolved\.__ Asymmetry resolution is faster than score elevation for many operators \(the deficit side has more room to gain\)\.  


__Tactical operator caveat:__ Operators accumulate predictable occupational asymmetries — dominant shooting side, dominant carry side, asymmetric ruck loading, body armor strap patterns\. Default the tactical baseline corrective dose toward T\-spine rotation, hip flexor length on the dominant kneeling side, posterior cuff health on the dominant arm, and ankle DF bilaterally\. Catch the asymmetries before they become injuries\.

## <a id="_49qqrxz6pzzp"></a>__\[CHUNK 18 — SESSION ARCHITECTURE: WHERE CORRECTIVES LIVE\]__

__Tags:__ programming, session\_template, corrective\_block, integration

Correctives live inside the session, not as homework you'll skip\. Two valid models:

__Model A — Embedded \(Boyle/Cressey standard\)\.__ Best for operators with 1–2 minor screens to clear\. Correctives slot into warm\-up and as fillers between main\-lift sets\. No standalone session needed\.

Standard 60–75 min session template:

1. __Soft tissue — 5–7 min\.__ Foam roller, lacrosse ball\. Target identified restrictions: calves, quads, glutes, lats, pec minor, T\-spine\.
2. __Joint mobility — 3–5 min\.__ Two or three Level 1 drills from the operator's failed screens\. Day\-specific — lower\-body day = ankle/hip/T\-spine; upper\-body day = T\-spine/shoulder\.
3. __Activation / static motor control — 4–6 min\.__ Two or three Level 2 drills\. Dead bug, half\-kneeling Pallof, no\-money drill, prone Y\-T\-W\.
4. __Dynamic prep — 3–5 min\.__ Level 3 drills\. Bear crawl, KB armbar, walk\-out push\-up, lunge matrix\.
5. __Main session — 35–45 min\.__ Strength and conditioning\. Embed Level 5 lifts\. Pair priority correctives as A2/B2 fillers between main\-lift sets\.
6. __Cool\-down — 5 min\.__ Static stretching of trained areas, breathing reset\.

__Model B — Movement maintenance day\.__ Best for operators with multiple 1s, asymmetries, or in deload weeks\. A standalone 30–45 min movement session 1x/week or on a recovery day\. Mobility \+ Level 2/3 stability work\. No high load\. Cook recommends this for high\-volume tactical operators in the field\.

__Day\-specific corrective selection:__

- __Squat\-focus day:__ ankle DF, hip flexion, T\-spine extension correctives\.
- __Hinge\-focus day:__ ASLR, toe touch, dead bug, hip flexor length\.
- __Upper push day:__ shoulder flexion, T\-spine, serratus/lower trap, anti\-extension core\.
- __Upper pull day:__ posterior cuff, scap retraction, T\-spine rotation\.
- __Conditioning day:__ rotary stability, single\-leg stance\.

__Volume and frequency rules:__

- __Mobility \(Level 1\):__ daily, 2–3x/day if possible\. Short, frequent doses outperform long, infrequent stretches\. Cook: "test it four times a day, don't train it\."
- __Static and dynamic stability \(Level 2–3\):__ 3–4x/week\. Quality over quantity\.
- __Pattern \(Level 4\):__ 2–3x/week as warm\-up A1\.
- __Load \(Level 5\):__ 1–2x/week per major lift\.

The corrective block is not extra credit\. It is the warm\-up\. Skip it and the gates stay locked\.

## <a id="_1mqfzwm9xalx"></a>__\[CHUNK 19 — RED LIGHT / YELLOW LIGHT / GREEN LIGHT FILTER\]__

__Tags:__ programming, exercise\_filter, locked\_movements, substitutes

Once the screen identifies a dysfunctional pattern, every exercise in the operator's programming gets filtered\.

__Red light — locked\.__ Exercises that load the dysfunctional pattern directly\. Remove them entirely until the screen clears\.

- ASLR dysfunctional \(1 or asymmetry\) → deadlift, KB swing, RDL, good morning red\.
- Shoulder Mobility dysfunctional → strict OHP, push press, jerk, snatch, bar pull\-ups under load red\.
- TSPU dysfunctional → loaded overhead pressing, weighted dips, bench press at heavy load red\.
- Rotary Stability dysfunctional → loaded rotational lifts, heavy bent\-over rows, heavy carries red\.
- Deep Squat dysfunctional → back squat, front squat at full depth red\.
- In\-Line Lunge dysfunctional → loaded walking lunge, Bulgarian split squat red\.
- Hurdle Step dysfunctional → loaded step\-up, single\-leg squat, pistol red\.

__Yellow light — proceed with caution\.__ Related but less direct loading\. Reduce intensity, add monitoring, regress the variant\.

- ASLR dysfunctional → KB deadlift to box \(limited ROM, lighter load\) yellow\.
- SM dysfunctional → landmine press \(angled, friendlier ROM\) yellow\.
- Deep Squat dysfunctional → goblet squat above parallel, heel\-elevated goblet squat yellow\.
- ILL dysfunctional → goblet reverse lunge unloaded or light yellow\.

__Green light — train freely\.__ Exercises that don't load the dysfunction and may correct it\.

- ASLR dysfunctional → upper\-body work, half\-kneeling drills, dead bug, T\-spine mobility green\.
- SM dysfunctional → lower\-body work, anti\-rotation core, hip mobility green\.
- Deep Squat dysfunctional → upper\-body work, single\-leg balance, anti\-rotation, hinge work IF ASLR clears green\.

Cook's principle: sometimes what we remove is more important than what we add\. Pulling the red\-lit exercise out of programming is itself the intervention\. Don't fill the slot with more red\-lit work in disguise\. Fill it with green\-lit work that addresses the screen\.

## <a id="_9i5yrfssd5mf"></a>__\[CHUNK 20 — TACTICAL POPULATION CONTEXT\]__

__Tags:__ tactical, military, occupational\_load, population\_norms

This corpus is calibrated to operators — military, first responder, contractor, tactical professional\. The screen logic is identical, but the population context shifts the read\.

__Normative data:__

- __Active\-duty service members \(Teyhen 2014, n=247\):__ mean composite 16\.2 ± 2\.2\. Aim above this baseline\.
- __Pre\-accession military applicants \(USMEPCOM cohort, n=1,714\):__ mean composite 14\.7 ± 1\.8\. A substantial subset enters service already at the ≤14 risk threshold\.
- __Service members <30 years old outperform ≥30 on FMS, ankle DF, Y\-Balance LQ, hop tests\.__ Age\-stratify expectations\.
- __Marine officer candidates \(O'Connor 2011, n=874\):__ 45\.8% of candidates with ≤14 sustained injury during officer training, vs 30\.6% of those >14\.

__Operational impact statistic:__ Musculoskeletal disorders accounted for ~24% of medical evacuations from OIF/OEF combat operations, vs ~14% for combat injuries\. Movement\-related injury is a larger force\-readiness threat than combat trauma\. The screen is part of force preservation, not gym vanity\.

__Stacked gating \(Lisman 2013\):__ FMS ≤14 \+ 3\-mile run >20\.5 min → 4\.2× injury risk in Marine officer candidates\. The hardest signal in the literature for tactical injury prediction comes from STACKING movement quality and aerobic capacity\. Operators below both thresholds get the heaviest restriction\.

__Predictable tactical asymmetries to screen for:__

- __Dominant shooting side:__ thoracic rotation asymmetry, posterior cuff overuse on dominant arm, anterior shoulder tightness\.
- __Dominant carry side:__ unilateral hip flexor shortness, lat shortness, lumbar rotation bias\.
- __Ruck loading:__ thoracic kyphosis, pec minor shortness, hip extension loss, ankle DF loss from boot wear\.
- __Body armor:__ chronic anterior chain shortness, T\-spine extension loss, breathing pattern dysfunction\.
- __Vehicle/seated time:__ hip flexor shortness, glute inhibition, ankle DF loss\.

Default the tactical baseline corrective dose toward: ankle DF, hip flexion, T\-spine extension and rotation, posterior cuff, anterior core anti\-extension\. These are the perennial weak links in the population\. NSCA TSAC standard\.

__Re\-screening in tactical environments:__

- Pre\-deployment baseline\.
- Post\-deployment re\-screen \(8–12 weeks back home, after acute decompression\)\.
- Post\-injury return\-to\-duty re\-screen\.
- Annual readiness baseline\.
- After any selection event, school, or major training cycle\.

__Honest caveat on the science:__ FMS sensitivity is modest\. A clean screen does not guarantee an uninjured operator\. The screen is a rule\-IN tool for elevated risk, not a rule\-OUT tool for safety\. Use it to gate load when it flags, not to clear operators when it doesn't\. Layer it with aerobic capacity, injury history, sleep, training load monitoring\. The gates in this corpus are necessary but not sufficient — they keep operators out of preventable injury, they don't make operators bulletproof\.

Move well\. Then move heavy\. Then move heavy under fatigue\. Then ruck\. In that order\. Every time\.
